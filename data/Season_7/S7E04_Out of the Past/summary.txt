The agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., after surviving a devastating confrontation with Izel and the loss of their original Coulson, are locked in a battle across time to thwart the Chronicoms—a race of synthetic beings intent on rewriting history and erasing S.H.I.E.L.D. from existence. Using a time-travel-equipped Zephyr, the team intervened in 1930s New York, saving Wilfred Malick (future Hydra leader) to preserve the timeline and—despite deep moral qualms—ensured Hydra's rise for the greater good. May is physically healed, but her psyche is altered, displaying unfamiliar emotional detachment, while Daisy and others struggle with the complex consequences of their actions and the presence of the Coulson LMD, whose identity and humanity remain a point of contention.

In 1955, the team stopped Chronicom sabotage at an Area 51 S.H.I.E.L.D. test, contending with gender and racial bias of the era, while May and Yo-Yo faced difficulties from their own impaired abilities. Agent Daniel Sousa, a legendary S.H.I.E.L.D. figure, began to suspect their true origins after nearly exposing Simmons' masquerade as Peggy Carter. The Zephyr next arrives at the day Sousa was fated to become S.H.I.E.L.D.'s first martyr, targeted by both human conspirators and the Chronicoms. Coulson, his LMD systems damaged and perception altered, connects with Sousa and improvises a new plan to save him. While Yo-Yo and Deke scramble to recover the vital S.H.I.E.L.D. device stolen by the Chronicoms, Deke is abducted by Malick’s men but spared due to past assistance. As the team shields Sousa from assassination—and history itself teeters on the brink—questions about fate, sacrifice, and the possibility of changing legacies dominate.

Now, Sousa is alive but increasingly aware he’s caught in a web of secrets, Hydra’s role is confirmed behind the historical events, and the Chronicoms remain a looming threat, targeting not only the organization's history but also its agents' very identities. The team remains fractured but determined, dogged by temporal uncertainties, May’s enigmatic condition, and the unresolved presence of Wilfred Malick, as they strive to protect the very foundation of S.H.I.E.L.D.—and the course of history itself.
