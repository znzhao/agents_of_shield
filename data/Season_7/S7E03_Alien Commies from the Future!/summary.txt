After their confrontation with Izel and Pachakutiq, which left Melinda May wounded and compelled them to rely on a Chronicom-enhanced LMD of Coulson, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team is thrust into a desperate game of time travel. Pursued by Chronicom Hunters aiming to erase S.H.I.E.L.D. from history, the team first lands in 1931 New York. There, they save Wilfred 'Freddy' Malick—who will become a key founder of Hydra—thus ensuring the beginnings of both S.H.I.E.L.D. and its ancient enemy remain on course. This mission forces the agents to grapple with their own moral boundaries, preserving a villain for the greater good, while Daisy and others wrestle with the paradox of protecting Hydra to save the world as they know it. May, meanwhile, is physically healed but psychologically altered, displaying emotional detachment and a muted response to traumatic events, including the return of "Coulson" as an LMD.

Shunted next to 1955 Nevada, the team intercepts the Chronicoms’ plot to sabotage Project Helius, an early S.H.I.E.L.D. attempt at an ion fusion generator, at the Area 51 base. Chronicom infiltration forces Simmons and Coulson to pose as Peggy Carter and a Department of Defense official to investigate, but their cover is nearly blown by Agent Daniel Sousa. As they and their allies navigate period sexism, racism, and their own impostor status, they deduce the Chronicoms intend to use Helius to create a devastating explosion, erasing top S.H.I.E.L.D. scientists from history. May and Yo-Yo’s attempt to stop the Chronicom infiltrators is hampered by May’s uncharacteristic panic attack and Yo-Yo’s recurring issues with her powers. After a chaotic confrontation, Simmons manages to activate an EMP that neutralizes both the Chronicoms and the LMD Coulson, thwarting the immediate threat—but not before Sousa becomes suspicious of their true origins. The team remains on the run, their unity strained by unresolved emotional scars and the relentless pressure of preserving S.H.I.E.L.D.'s legacy against a time-bending enemy.
