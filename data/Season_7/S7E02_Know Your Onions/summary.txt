The S.H.I.E.L.D. team, reeling from their fierce battle with Izel and Pachakutiq—which left May gravely wounded and led to an alliance of necessity with a Chronicom-enhanced LMD of Phil Coulson—find themselves thrust into 1931 New York. The Chronicoms, seeking to ensure their own survival by erasing S.H.I.E.L.D. from history, are targeting a moment critical to both S.H.I.E.L.D.'s creation and Hydra's rise: the fate of the young Wilfred 'Freddy' Malick. With Simmons and Enoch's improvised time travel technology, the team must now ensure Malick's survival, even knowing his role as the forebear of HYDRA's modern leadership, so that the battle between S.H.I.E.L.D. and Hydra—and the future itself—can unfold as it should.

Mack and Deke take Malick under their protection, dodging the police and the Chronicoms while uncovering that Malick's delivery is not mere alcohol but a mysterious chemical agent integral to Dr. Erskine's future Super Soldier Serum. Meanwhile, Simmons, Daisy, and Yo-Yo work with Ernest Koenig in his speakeasy to treat Malick's Hydra contact and deduce Freddy's true mission. Their moral dilemma intensifies as they grasp that preserving S.H.I.E.L.D.'s existence means ensuring Hydra's foundation. Simultaneously, Enoch struggles to manage a physically recovered but emotionally detached May, who exhibits numb detachment—especially upon seeing "Coulson" revived as an LMD. As the team regroups aboard Zephyr One, they recognize the high stakes of their time travel gambit: they must act as both protectors and reluctant architects of history's darkness to safeguard the greater good, all while racing against the increasingly desperate Chronicom Hunters.
