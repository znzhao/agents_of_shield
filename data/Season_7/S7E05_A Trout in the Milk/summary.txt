The agents of S.H.I.E.L.D., alongside the LMD version of Coulson and Agent Sousa (whose life they recently saved, altering his historical fate), are pursuing the Chronicoms through time to stop their attempts to rewrite S.H.I.E.L.D.'s history and ensure Hydra's infiltration remains intact to avoid disastrous changes. Their journey has forced the team into morally gray choices—like sparing and later confronting Wilfred Malick and preserving Hydra's rise—all while wrestling with the unpredictable nature of time travel and the psychological toll it takes, especially on those like May, who now experiences emotions only through others, and Yo-Yo, whose powers appear mysteriously compromised.

Transported to 1973 New York, the team discovers Project Insight in play decades ahead of schedule, orchestrated by Wilfred Malick and his son Nathaniel with the help of the Chronicoms. The early launch targets potential future threats to Hydra—like Bruce Banner—and would irreparably distort history. The team splits up: Coulson and May attempt infiltration, Daisy and Sousa uncover shocking evidence of the altered timeline, and Mack and Yo-Yo discover that Malick and the Chronicoms are leveraging Mack's parents, now prisoners in the Lighthouse, against them. Deke fatally shoots Wilfred Malick before learning the full extent of the Chronicoms' plans. Facing the impossible choice between saving Mack's family and stopping Insight, the agents improvise a new plan, destroying the satellite—but exposing themselves to S.H.I.E.L.D. in the process.

Relationships are strained by these harrowing events—Simmons feels she is "slipping" without Enoch's constant presence for support, Daisy grows closer to Sousa as he grapples with his place outside his time, and May's emotional detachment continues to impact her interactions. With Coulson and May now captured in the Lighthouse, the team's position compromised, the timeline deeply unstable, and the Chronicoms still at large, the fate of S.H.I.E.L.D. and its legacy hangs by a thread—complicated further by the unpredictable ripples of every decision they make in the past.
