The struggle between the agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. and the Chronicoms intensified as both groups navigated through rapidly shifting time periods, each seeking to reshape history to their advantage. The S.H.I.E.L.D. team, forced to make painful decisions, sometimes aided the ascension of Hydra to ensure the timeline's preservation and even eliminated key historical figures like Wilfred Malick. Tragedy struck as Mack witnessed his parents' replacement and murder by Chronicom duplicates, causing him to spiral into grief and isolation. During this period, Daisy and Sousa were subjected to brutal experiments by Nathaniel Malick, who gained quake powers and established himself as a new threat. Tensions within the team mounted as Simmons, whose deteriorating memory (due to self-imposed blocks hiding Fitz's location) sowed further distrust, struggled to provide answers and leadership, while Coulson continuously sacrificed his own existence—at one stage becoming an AI—to help the others and fight the Chronicoms.

After a failed mission stranded Mack and Deke in the 1980s, Mack withdrew, grieving and lost, while Deke adapted to their new reality, forming the "Deke Squad" as a cover for covert S.H.I.E.L.D. operations. When the timeline skipped again, leaving most of the team separated for over a year (from their own perspective a mere hour), Deke and Mack successfully fought off Chronicom threats even at personal cost, regaining purpose and camaraderie before the main team managed to reconnect with them. The Chronicoms, led by Sibyl, remained at large with access to the powerful Time Stream, ensuring the timeline's volatility and continued endangerment.

As the Zephyr's time drive began to fail, threatening to collapse the ship into a singularity, the team raced to fix it. Yo-Yo, believing her Inhuman speed might be the key to navigating the deadly field around the time drive, accepted that her powers were psychologically blocked. Accompanied by May, she traveled to Afterlife to seek help from Jiaying, Daisy's mother, where she confronted childhood trauma at the root of her limitations. Meanwhile, Afterlife itself was thrown into chaos as Nathaniel Malick recruited Jiaying's unstable daughter Kora after she violently lashed out, setting the stage for new alliances and betrayals. Amid tragedy and battle at Afterlife, Yo-Yo began to face her inner obstacles, but Kora—now empowered and emboldened—committed herself to Malick's path, leaving Afterlife in ruins and S.H.I.E.L.D. with even greater threats to overcome as the team raced out of time.
