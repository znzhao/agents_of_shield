After S.H.I.E.L.D. traces the Chronicoms through various historical eras, the agents are repeatedly forced to make difficult moral choices to protect the timeline—even when it means safeguarding Hydra’s rise or taking lethal action against figures like Wilfred Malick. Interpersonal strains grow as Mack must grapple with the endangerment—and, ultimately, the tragic loss via Chronicom duplications—of his parents, while Daisy and Sousa, now increasingly close, are both captured and subjected to Nathaniel Malick’s sinister experiments to transfer Daisy’s Inhuman powers. May continues to struggle with her altered empathy, feeling emotions only through physical contact, as Simmons secretly wrestles with a memory suppressant meant to conceal Fitz's whereabouts while he guides the team covertly.

The Chronicoms, adapting with each setback, move to infiltrate S.H.I.E.L.D. at the highest level: replacing agents and even Mack’s parents with their own, launching a direct assault from within. General Stoner learns the true stakes as Coulson and May expose a Chronicom plot inside the Lighthouse. Coulson, demonstrating his resolve and self-sacrifice, interfaces with the Chronicom Predictor Sibyl and ultimately detonates the hidden Chronicom vessel, taking out their Hunter forces but perishing in the process. Meanwhile, the Zephyr is critically damaged, Simmons’s memory link to Fitz falters, and a grieving Mack is forced to eliminate the duplicates wearing his parents’ faces. The team is left battered and uncertain, with their ship compromised, alliances shaken, and the Chronicoms redoubling their efforts to rewrite history, leaving the fate of S.H.I.E.L.D.—and time itself—dangling by a thread.
