The S.H.I.E.L.D. team has been desperately battling the Chronicoms, robotic beings intent on erasing S.H.I.E.L.D. from history and conquering Earth, as the agents are flung through ever-changing time periods by the malfunctioning Zephyr One time drive. Their struggle has forced them to make morally grey decisions, including enabling Hydra's rise and killing historical figures to maintain the timeline. The Chronicoms, led by Sibyl, manipulate both time and key individuals such as Nathaniel Malick, who acquires quake powers for himself and recruits other dangerous Inhumans, including Kora, Daisy's half-sister, adding new threats to the timeline. The internal pressure mounts as Simmons hides her knowledge of Fitz's location behind a memory-blocking implant, further eroding team trust, while Mack's trauma over his parents' deaths and Yo-Yo's struggle with her power block add to the emotional burden. Deke adapts to leadership in the 1980s, showing personal growth, and Sousa gradually integrates and bonds with Daisy.

In their latest crisis, the Zephyr becomes trapped in an apocalyptic time storm, rapidly approaching destruction in a temporal vortex. Daisy and Coulson are the only ones able to retain memory through lethal time loops, forcing them to witness their friends' repeated deaths and investigate the cause. They discover that Simmons has subconsciously programmed Enoch to kill anyone who tries to remove her implant, a safeguard against revealing Fitz's hiding place. Only by confronting Enoch and convincing him to sacrifice his own life—removing a vital part to repair the ship—do they escape annihilation, but not without profound loss. Enoch's death and last warning that this will be the team's final mission together cast a sobering shadow over their survival, as the Zephyr narrowly escapes the time storm but at the price of a beloved ally. The team remains battered, both physically and emotionally, with many mysteries, especially surrounding Fitz and the ultimate plan against the Chronicoms, still unresolved.
