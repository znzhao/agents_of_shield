The S.H.I.E.L.D. team, hunted through history by the time-manipulating Chronicoms and their deadly human ally Nathaniel Malick, has been forced to make grim choices to protect the timeline and thwart the enemy’s plans to erase S.H.I.E.L.D. from existence. The team’s journey has brought deep personal costs: moral compromises, shaken loyalties, and the loss of friends like Enoch, whose sacrifice saved them from annihilation in the time storm, but also foretold the end of the team's era together. Jemma Simmons’ secret knowledge about Fitz's location, protected by a memory suppression device, strains trust among her allies, while Daisy’s relationship with her long-lost family remains fraught and uncertain, compounded by unclear consequences to the timeline due to their interventions.

Nathaniel Malick has found ways to steal and redistribute Inhuman powers, recruiting dangerous individuals—including Daisy’s half-sister Kora and newly turning John Garrett—with promises of power and anarchist ideology. In a devastating turn, Malick and Garrett infiltrate S.H.I.E.L.D. strongholds, and leveraging Daisy’s own quake ability, Malick murders Jiaying in front of her daughter. Daisy, reeling from this, is left grieving and enraged, while trust continues to erode among the core team: Daisy’s secrets about her family surface, and feelings of guilt and frustration deepen between colleagues, especially after May and Yo-Yo withheld critical information. The team remains fractured and deeply wounded as Malick, with a swelling roster of superpowered allies, grows bolder, the Chronicoms’ ultimate plan remains unclear, and Fitz’s location remains the pivotal mystery that could tip the scales of their final conflict.
