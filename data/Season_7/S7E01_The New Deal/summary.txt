After Phil Coulson's death, S.H.I.E.L.D. faced devastating threats: first from the mysterious Sarge, a being with Coulson's appearance, and then the non-corporeal Izel. Izel’s plan to unleash her people across dimensions nearly destroyed the team, pushing every agent to the brink. Melinda May, mortally wounded in a world-between-worlds, managed to help defeat both Izel and Sarge (revealed as Pachakutiq, a being spawned from Monolith energy) at considerable personal cost. The aftermath left May critically injured, Yo-Yo barely rescued from a Shrike infection, and FitzSimmons separated—Fitz’s fate uncertain as the Chronicoms leveraged S.H.I.E.L.D.'s knowledge for their own agenda. With Izel vanquished but the Chronicom Hunters advancing, Earth's fate hung by a thread.

The Chronicoms, intent on erasing S.H.I.E.L.D. to secure Earth as their new home, forced Simmons and Enoch to devise desperate countermeasures. The team found themselves unexpectedly transported to 1931 New York City, tasked with thwarting Chronicom hunters from erasing S.H.I.E.L.D.'s very foundation. Simmons activated a Chronicom-enhanced LMD Phil Coulson, granting the team his insight but burdening him with overwhelming, rapidly updated memories. Dispersed and disoriented, the agents investigated a grotesque murder where Chronicoms stole police officers' faces, infiltrated a speakeasy using familiar S.H.I.E.L.D. passcodes, and posed as Canadian Mounties to pursue their adversaries. The agents discovered the target was not Franklin D. Roosevelt but the young, impressionable Wilfred 'Freddy' Malick—future father of notorious Hydra leader Gideon Malick. Realizing that saving S.H.I.E.L.D. requires ensuring Hydra's rise so that S.H.I.E.L.D. can exist to oppose it, the agents are faced with a morally fraught mission, fighting not only to preserve their own history but the entire fate of humanity.
