The fight against the Chronicoms and Nathaniel Malick has reached its breaking point, with S.H.I.E.L.D.'s core team battered both physically and emotionally as they battle to prevent the organization's erasure from history. Their time-hopping confrontation with the Chronicoms has resulted in heavy losses, strained alliances, and the near-total destruction of S.H.I.E.L.D.'s strongholds, leaving only the resilient Lighthouse operational. Trust has been eroded, especially due to the painful secrets surrounding Fitz's whereabouts—with Simmons' memories of Fitz forcibly erased—while Daisy's fraught reunion with her sister Kora, manipulated by Malick, deepens her personal struggle. Malick's alliance with the Chronicoms and acquisition of Inhuman powers have made him a formidable threat, and the Chronicoms have now launched a full-scale assault from their warship, directly targeting the remaining agents.

As the Zephyr One is taken into the Chronicoms’ control, Daisy, Mack, and Sousa mount a risky rescue of Simmons and Deke amid an escalating space conflict. Simmons is injected with a compound to dissolve her memory implant, further scrambling her recollections of Fitz, while Malick and Sibyl exploit every avenue to find his location. Daisy reaches Simmons and Deke, narrowly evades Kora by opting to appeal to her humanity instead of fighting, and manages to escape back to the Zephyr. Meanwhile, Mack and Sousa repurpose destroyed Chronicom Hunters into missiles to blow their way free. On Earth, Coulson, May, and Yo-Yo survive an attack on the Lighthouse and turn an enemy—John Garrett—to their side when Malick betrays him. Using Garrett’s newly aligned abilities, they teleport to Koenig’s bar, following S.H.I.E.L.D.'s last lead for hope. With the S.H.I.E.L.D. team fractured, memories lost, and their historical legacy on the verge of annihilation, all hinges on reuniting, overcoming betrayal, and discovering Fitz's fate before time runs out.
