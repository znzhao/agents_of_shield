After being transported to a dystopian future where the shattered remnants of humanity are enslaved by the Kree in the Lighthouse, the core S.H.I.E.L.D. team is scattered, forced to adapt to brutal new circumstances. Simmons is pressed into servitude for the Kree leader Kasius, May is stranded on the devastated surface, and Daisy is captured and rendered powerless. Fitz manages a daring arrival from the past and helps instigate a series of jailbreaks and uprisings, aided by Deke, Flint (a new Inhuman with matter manipulation abilities), and embattled survivors like Tess. Unraveling Robin Hinton's cryptic prophecies, the team realizes that reversing Earth's destruction hinges on manipulating the timeline from within this fractured future, with Flint's powers central to their escape.

As resistance strengthens, key losses (including Robin's death) and gruesome revelations—such as the Kree's experimentation on Inhumans and Yo-Yo's own future corpse being repeatedly revived and harvested—heighten the stakes. The reunited S.H.I.E.L.D. agents coordinate a final stand: Coulson, May, Daisy, and the others liberate captive Inhumans and civilians while Flint reassembles the Monolith that can return them to their time. Kasius resorts to using odium to empower himself and assassinates the future Yo-Yo, but Mack and Simmons overpower him at great risk. Facing overwhelming odds and Kree reprisals, Deke and Enoch stay behind to ensure the Monolith is powered, sacrificing themselves by detonating Enoch’s power supply to open the time portal as the Lighthouse is engulfed in explosions. The fate of the team now hinges on this desperate leap back to the past, shadowed by the uncertainty of changing or repeating the world's catastrophic fate.
