After the defeat of Aida and the fallout from the Framework, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team—Coulson, Daisy, May, Simmons, Mack, and Yo-Yo—found themselves abducted by unknown operatives wielding advanced technology and transported by a mysterious monolith. Awakening in a strange space station, they quickly realize they are not only far from home, but have also been flung into the distant future. Here, they discover that Earth has been shattered and humanity now barely survives under the oppressive rule of the Kree within the confines of a station known as the Lighthouse. The Kree maintain control through advanced tech, brutal enforcement, and the erasure of historical records, keeping people focused solely on survival and servitude.

The team struggles to adapt: Coulson, May, and Simmons attempt to blend in with help from scavenger Deke, who operates a black-market Framework simulation as an escape for suffering humans. Deke, suspicious and motivated by self-interest, reluctantly assists after the death of his associate Virgil—one of the few who believed in legends about S.H.I.E.L.D.'s return as humanity's saviors. The group learns about the rigid, violent hierarchy enforced by the Kree, including the deadly "renewal" process where low earners are forced to kill or be killed. Simmons is singled out and taken by Kasius, the local Kree leader, who seems to take a particular interest in her intellect, potentially putting her at grave risk. Meanwhile, the team struggles with trust issues and desperate alliances, barely surviving the renewal while Tess, a local ally, helps to shield them under the guise of working for the ruthless foreman Grill. As they try to regroup, the agents face mounting peril and daunting questions: how did Earth fall, can they return to the past, and can they free this last bastion of humanity from Kree domination?
