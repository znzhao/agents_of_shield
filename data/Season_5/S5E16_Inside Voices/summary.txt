The S.H.I.E.L.D. team, having escaped a dystopian future where Earth is destroyed, returns to the present only to face new and greater threats. The destruction of the Monoliths has caused rifts in reality, forcing the team to confront their traumas as dangers from other realities break through. This ordeal exacerbates personal rifts: Fitz’s psyche fractures, leading him to resurrect his ruthless alter ego and betray Daisy’s trust; Yo-Yo, newly equipped with prosthetic arms after a traumatic injury, struggles with her sense of self and destiny, while Mack supports her despite their differing beliefs about fate. Simmons, reeling from Fitz’s actions and learning that Deke is her and Fitz’s future grandson, becomes convinced of her own invincibility due to their survival in the alternate timeline, an idea Yo-Yo strongly supports.

General Hale, a surviving Hydra operative, advances her plan to create a super-powered champion for Earth, using gravitonium and alien technology acquired through deals with a mysterious alien Confederacy that offers protection from an impending extraterrrestrial invasion. Capturing Coulson and General Talbot, Hale attempts to coerce their compliance, subjecting Talbot to brainwashing and torturing Coulson for information. She tests the powers of gravitonium on Creel, who is overwhelmed by psychic visions of people previously absorbed into the element and becomes convinced it is alive. Meanwhile, Daisy, May, and Deke locate Robin Hinton, whose precognitive abilities may provide clues to Coulson’s whereabouts, while Simmons, Yo-Yo, and Fitz orchestrate a ruse to escape the Lighthouse, exploiting Mack’s trust and their belief in their own survival.

As internal trust within the team continues to fray, Coulson manages to escape Hale’s compound with Talbot, aided by Creel. They use an alien teleportation device to land in a snowy wilderness, guided perhaps by Robin’s visions. With Hale still pursuing her dangerous agenda and S.H.I.E.L.D. fractured by distrust and existential uncertainty, the fate of Earth—and the line between saving and destroying it—hang in the balance.
