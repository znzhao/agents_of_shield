After narrowly escaping a dystopian future where Earth was shattered, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team returns to the present—only to find themselves grappling with fractured trust and mounting existential threats. The destruction of the Monoliths has weakened the barriers between realities, while General Hale, acting under the Confederacy’s threat of alien invasion, works to create an Earth-saving champion using gravitonium and alien technology. Fitz’s psychological breakdowns and resurrection of his darker side sow divisions with Daisy and Mack, while Simmons and Yo-Yo, convinced the future cannot be changed, adopt increasingly reckless mindsets. Deke, their descendant from the future, becomes an unlikely part of the team as they struggle to discern fate from free will. Meanwhile, Coulson is kidnapped and tortured by Hale, an event that further strains his relationships—especially with May, as she challenges his self-sacrificial instincts.

The team splits to confront various dangers: Daisy and May pursue a Hydra bunker in the Appalachians after tracing Hydra mech activity, while FitzSimmons are forced by Ruby and Werner von Strucker to reconstruct the gravitonium infusion chamber in a Hydra base in England. Internal chaos grows as General Talbot, traumatized and secretly under Hydra's Faustus brainwashing, abducts Robin—the young seer whose future visions are crucial—before being subdued by Coulson and Mack. Meanwhile, Ruby’s attempt to infuse herself with gravitonium proves catastrophic; she is driven mad by the voices of those trapped within and her uncontrolled powers kill Werner. Efforts to reason with her by Daisy and her mother fail, culminating in Yo-Yo killing Ruby to prevent further destruction. Stunned by the cost, the team is forced to question whether the fated cycle of disaster has truly been averted, all while General Hale escapes, now vengeful and more desperate than ever. With Ruby dead, S.H.I.E.L.D.'s unity in shambles, and gravitonium still uncontained, the world remains perched on the edge of ruin.
