After escaping a dystopian future by shattering the time-altering Monoliths, the core S.H.I.E.L.D. team returns to a present beset by chaos and internal conflict. The threat of alien invasion intensifies as the Confederacy—a council of powerful alien leaders—targets Earth and demands submission, manipulating Hydra and leveraging the planet’s precious gravitonium. S.H.I.E.L.D. is bitterly divided: Yo-Yo’s fatal intervention with Ruby causes deep rifts, especially with Mack and Daisy; Fitz wrestles with the consequences of his darker persona’s actions; Simmons clings to hope that the timeline is fixed; and Deke, their accidental descendant, searches for connection in a world where his own history is erased. General Talbot, traumatized by both Hydra’s brainwashing and his own failure, volunteers as a last resort for heroic redemption and infuses himself with all remaining gravitonium, gaining immense and unstable powers.

With the Lighthouse—the team’s stronghold—under siege by Remorath marauders, S.H.I.E.L.D. fends off the invaders at great personal and moral cost. Talbot uses his new abilities to save the team but becomes increasingly erratic and power-hungry, forcefully seizing control aboard the Remorath mothership. Accompanied by Coulson, Talbot asserts dominance over Qovas and demands a place in the Confederacy, even consuming one of its members to prove his might. The Confederacy’s true intentions are revealed by the formidable Kree, Taryan: they never planned to defend Earth from Thanos, only to steal its resources. Taryan manipulates Talbot, urging him to claim more gravitonium from beneath the Earth’s surface, sowing seeds of megalomania. Meanwhile, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team reels from loss, distrust, and fracturing loyalty, as Daisy prepares a dangerous rescue and considers using her mother Jiaying’s remains with the Centipede Device as a last-ditch effort to heal Coulson, knowing it may cross moral lines.

Unresolved tensions and moral boundaries threaten to splinter the surviving agents, while Talbot’s gravitonium-induced spiral sets the stage for Earth’s possible destruction—from within. The Confederacy’s manipulation, Earth’s vulnerability to cosmic threats, and the team’s dissolved unity leave the fate of humanity hanging in the balance, with the most dangerous threat now one of their own.
