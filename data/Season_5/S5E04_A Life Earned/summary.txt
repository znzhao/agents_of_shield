After narrowly escaping Aida and the Framework, the core S.H.I.E.L.D. team is abruptly thrust into a dystopian future, where Earth's remnants are under the harsh rule of Kree overlords inside the Lighthouse space habitat. Humanity lives in misery, constantly surveilled and controlled, as the Kree exploit Inhuman abilities for profit and entertainment. Simmons is abducted and forcibly serves Kasius, the Kree leader running gladiatorial contests to present and sell Inhumans—a painful reality highlighted when a terrified young Inhuman is made to kill for Kree amusement. Daisy is also captured and becomes known as "Quake, Destroyer of Worlds," a mythical figure blamed for Earth's destruction. The team fragments further when Daisy's solo rescue attempt results in her capture, and local survivor Deke betrays her to preserve the lives of the downtrodden populace, haunted by his parents' fates at the hands of the Kree for resisting.

Coulson, May, Mack, and Yo-Yo struggle to navigate the cruel new order, taking dangerous risks to decode Virgil's message and survive the brutal work demanded by Grill, their Kree-aligned supervisor. The discovery that Kree commodify human infants based on Inhuman potential deepens the team's horror and disillusionment. Internal tensions rise, particularly for Mack, who is confronted with grief over his lost daughter, and for Coulson, whose hope is further strained by Deke's pragmatic betrayal. Meanwhile, Simmons and Daisy are forced to collaborate under Kasius' interrogation, helped only by the secret telepathic talents of Inhuman prisoner Ben. As May is captured in a fight against the Kree enforcer Sinara and the rest of the team is under harsh scrutiny or forced into moral compromise, the agents are isolated and powerless, with their unity, purpose, and hope tested by Kree oppression and the shattered remnants of humanity’s future.
