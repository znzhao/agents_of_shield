After narrowly escaping a future where Earth is destroyed and ruled by the Kree, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team returns to the present. The destruction of the Monoliths has destabilized space-time, causing a rift in reality which manifests physical threats and specters from the team's past traumas. As they race to contain the rift with gravitonium, personal and external threats converge. Yo-Yo struggles to recover mentally and physically after losing her arms, now equipped with prototype replacements. Mack is determined to support her through her pain, while Deke, a survivor from the destroyed timeline and adapting to this world, becomes a surprising asset to the team and gradually reveals he is Fitz and Simmons' grandson—something he discloses only to Simmons in a vulnerable moment.

Meanwhile, General Hale, leading a covert military faction, forms an uneasy alliance with dangerous individuals, including Carl Creel and Anton Ivanov, and coerces Werner von Strucker into assisting her with mysterious plans involving gravitonium and super-powered soldiers. Hale intentionally allows herself to be captured, only to flip the tables and force Coulson to come with her, buying the team's safety.

Under mounting stress, Fitz's psychological fractures resurface, culminating in his alter ego, "The Doctor" from the Framework, seizing control. Fitz's splintered mind, not a product of the rift but of his own trauma and exhaustion, orchestrates a brutal solution: he forcibly removes Daisy's power inhibitor and coerces her into using her quake abilities to seal the rift with gravitonium. Fitz later admits responsibility for his actions and claims he believes them necessary, leaving Simmons devastated and isolated from the man she loves. The team, reeling from this betrayal, must now deal with the fallout of Fitz's actions, Daisy's shaken trust, and looming uncertainties about Hale's agenda—and their own ability to stay united in the face of existential threats.
