The S.H.I.E.L.D. team found themselves mysteriously abducted from a diner and transported far into a dystopian future, where Earth has been shattered and humanity's remnants are subjugated by Kree overlords in a space habitat known as the Lighthouse. The Kree, led by Kasius and his deadly enforcer Sinara, exploit surviving humans—especially Inhumans, whose abilities are commodified and used for cruel entertainment or sale. The agents are scattered and powerless in this harsh regime: Simmons is forced into servitude for Kasius, Daisy is captured and branded as the infamous "Destroyer of Worlds," May is felled in combat and taken prisoner, and the rest are pushed into brutal labor. The team endures moral and emotional strain, with old grief and new betrayals (notably by pragmatic survivor Deke, who sacrifices Daisy to protect the desperate community) driving wedges between them. Their hope dims further upon discovering the Kree's breeding and evaluation of human infants for future Inhuman potential.

Meanwhile, back in the present, Fitz is left behind, captured and interrogated by the military under suspicion of involvement in his teammates' disappearance. Using subterfuge and the help of Lance Hunter, Fitz escapes custody and uncovers the truth: an alien Chronicom named Enoch orchestrated the team's abduction at the behest of a young Inhuman Seer, Robin Hinton, who possesses visions of Earth's destruction. With the military closing in to exploit Robin, Fitz, Hunter, Robin, and her mother are secretly spirited away by Enoch to the dormant Lighthouse. There, Fitz learns he was excluded from the original time-jump to fulfill a vital role in saving his friends. Enoch reveals that Fitz can follow the others into the future via a hidden pod—currently held in a military facility—prompting a daring infiltration and escape. Fitz departs alone for the future, determined to reunite with his team and help change the course of a devastated humanity, even as the Kree tighten their hold over the last fragments of Earth's survivors.
