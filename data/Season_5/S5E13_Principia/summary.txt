After returning from a bleak, Kree-dominated future, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team is beset by consequences rippling through their present. The simultaneous destruction of three Monoliths has destabilized space-time, manifesting dangerous anomalies and nightmares from their past. While battling these threats, the team learns Coulson is dying from the aftereffects of wielding the Ghost Rider spirit—a secret Simmons uncovers. Yo-Yo survives a brutal attack that costs her both arms, echoing tragedies she endured in the future and drawing her closer to Mack, who is determined to support her recovery. Deke, a survivor from the future, adapts to this world and becomes a key asset, while also discovering a shocking personal connection to Fitz and Simmons.

S.H.I.E.L.D. races to find more gravitonium to contain the unstable rift threatening the world. Their search leads them to Mack's old Academy roommate, Tony Caine, who reveals that the last known gravitonium shipment was lost with the ship Principia—now found mysteriously floating in the sky due to the gravitonium's effects. The team retrieves a gravitonium core from the wreckage despite interference from General Hale's advanced mechs. Meanwhile, General Hale and her daughter Ruby seek out Werner von Strucker, coercing him for his inherited expertise about his father's science, aiming to build a team of their own to challenge S.H.I.E.L.D.. As alliances form and tensions flare, the team regroups at the Lighthouse—Yo-Yo now gifted cybernetic arms made from their enemies—still haunted by the threat of the rift, General Hale's mysterious agenda, and the ever-present specter of Earth's potential destruction.
