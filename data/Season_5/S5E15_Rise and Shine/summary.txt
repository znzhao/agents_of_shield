The S.H.I.E.L.D. team, having barely escaped a future where Earth is destroyed—seemingly at Daisy Johnson's hand—returns to a present destabilized by the destruction of the Monoliths, which tear open a rift in reality manifesting physical threats from their deepest fears and traumas. As the team struggles to seal the rift with gravitonium, Fitz's psyche fractures under the strain, causing his ruthless alter-ego to resurface and force Daisy to use her powers, fracturing team trust. Relationships are strained: Simmons reels from Fitz's actions; Yo-Yo wrestles with trauma and her new prosthetics, supported by Mack; and Deke reveals to Simmons that he is their grandson from the destroyed timeline, finding a tenuous place among the team.

Meanwhile, General Hale emerges as a major antagonist, revealed to be the last survivor of Hydra and leader of a secret program to create super-powered soldiers using gravitonium and alien technology. Her backstory unfolds: a prodigy groomed by Whitehall, Hale was entrusted with a plan involving the Particle Infusion Chamber and her daughter Ruby, engineered to be Hydra’s new champion. In the present, after capturing Coulson and breaking General Talbot through torture to extract S.H.I.E.L.D.'s secrets, Hale brings Coulson before the alien Confederacy, who warn of an impending extraterrestrial invasion and demand gravitonium and Inhumans in exchange for protection. Hale's plan turns on using the Particle Infusion Chamber to infuse either Ruby or Daisy with gravitonium, aiming to create the "Destroyer of Worlds" as humanity's defender—but Coulson realizes this is the very act that leads to Earth's destruction in the future.

Coulson tries to warn Hale, but she remains deaf to his cautions, seeing S.H.I.E.L.D. only as an asset for her own agenda. Ruby, feeling overshadowed, becomes further embittered. As the threat of alien attack looms, S.H.I.E.L.D. is splintered by distrust and personal demons, while Hale’s ambition and the Confederacy’s hidden motives put the fate of Earth in dangerous hands. Talbot, broken and guilt-ridden, is used as a pawn by Hydra, and the true path to either salvation or destruction remains uncertain as the cost of misplaced trust and fractured alliances rises ever higher.
