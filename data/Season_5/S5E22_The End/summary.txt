S.H.I.E.L.D. has endured multiple existential threats: after escaping a dystopian future set in a shattered Earth ruled by alien Monolith forces, the agents face new challenges in the present. The team is fractured by trauma and moral dilemmas—especially after Yo-Yo kills Ruby, damaging her bond with Mack, while Fitz and Simmons grapple with the consequences of fate versus free will. General Talbot, traumatized by past brainwashing, volunteers to absorb gravitonium to save the world but is subsequently corrupted, convinced by Kree manipulator Taryan that seizing Earth's gravitonium core is the only way to ensure salvation. His transformation into "Quake's equal" positions him as an unstable, godlike threat.

Internal strife peaks when the only dose of Centipede serum, also the team's last hope of saving Coulson, becomes a lever in deciding whether to prioritize their leader's life or the world. After bitter debate and May's destruction of the Odium (which could have been used against Talbot), the team agrees to weaponize the serum for Earth's sake. Deke, unsure if he still exists in the new timeline, quietly departs. When Talbot targets Chicago, using his powers to extract gravitonium from beneath the city and causing widespread chaos, the team splits to rescue hostages and confront Talbot. Daisy, empowered by the Centipede serum which Coulson covertly leaves her, confronts Talbot, using her amplified abilities to defeat him by quaking him into space, averting Earth's destruction.

Yet victory comes at great cost. Fitz is mortally wounded during the rescue mission, dying in the rubble as Mack and May grieve beside him. Deke’s room is found empty, suggesting his possible erasure from existence. While the world is saved, the team is left shattered by loss—Coulson's condition remains terminal, and Simmons mourns both Fitz and the uncertain fate of Deke. Together, they memorialize Fitz, facing a future permanently changed by their sacrifices and fractured relationships.
