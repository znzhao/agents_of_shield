After the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D. at Hydra's hands, Coulson struggles to hold the fractured organization together while facing threats on multiple fronts. The team’s investigation into an alien artifact called the Diviner leads to the discovery of a hidden Kree city and the revelation that the Diviner is a Terrigen crystal, capable of triggering transformations in select humans with Inhuman ancestry. Skye and Raina undergo Terrigenesis, emerging with dangerous new powers but at a terrible cost: Agent Trip’s death shakes the group, and Skye is left traumatized, isolated by fear of her abilities and distrust from her colleagues. Fitz hides the evidence of Skye’s altered DNA to protect her from increasing paranoia within the team, especially from Simmons, who argues for strict containment or even elimination of powered threats.

While Coulson chases off Hydra operatives and faces S.H.I.E.L.D.’s dwindling resources, unrest brews from within. Bobbi Morse and Mack secretly report to an as-yet-unknown "real S.H.I.E.L.D.", while Hunter grows suspicious and is eventually taken prisoner to be brought before their leadership. Meanwhile, Skye’s father, Cal, assembles a revenge-driven team of former Gifted Index individuals—Karla Faye Gideon, Wendell Levi, Francis Noche, and the dangerously persuasive Angar—each with a personal grudge against S.H.I.E.L.D. Cal’s group attacks, seeking to make a public spectacle and draw Coulson out, culminating in a tense showdown in Coulson’s Wisconsin hometown. However, their plan is derailed when the teleporting Inhuman Gordon suddenly abducts Cal, causing confusion and allowing S.H.I.E.L.D. to subdue the other threats.

Through this turmoil, Skye’s psychological strain grows. Assigned to Dr. Andrew Garner, May’s ex-husband, for evaluation, Skye tries to prove her stability, but an emergency confrontation with her father only exacerbates her condition. Skye’s attempt to suppress her powers internally manifests as dangerous bruising and fractures, forcing Dr. Garner to recommend she leave S.H.I.E.L.D. until she gains control, even as May resists the idea. The team remains riven by secrets, fear, and the emergence of new, powerful superhuman threats, all while an internal splinter faction—the so-called "real S.H.I.E.L.D."—prepares to openly challenge Coulson’s leadership. Meanwhile, the fate of Cal, the nature of the hidden Inhuman community, and the threat of further Terrigenesis remain open mysteries.
