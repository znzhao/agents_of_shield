Following the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D. due to Hydra's infiltration, Coulson leads a diminished, covert team—Melinda May, Skye, Fitz, Simmons (undercover), Triplett, and others—dedicated to containing Hydra's growing threat and securing powerful alien technology. Trust within the team is fragile: Fitz is recovering from brain trauma, Simmons is deep undercover at Hydra risking exposure, and Ward, revealed as a Hydra agent, is now imprisoned but serves as a hesitant informant. Hydra, under Daniel Whitehall and his lieutenant Bakshi, aggressively pursues the Obelisk, an alien artifact capable of killing or transforming those who touch it, aiming to weaponize its effects for mass murder.

The mysterious alien symbols connected with the Obelisk become a focal point as Coulson is consumed with carving them, mirroring the fate of Garrett, his late predecessor who also craved the same symbols after GH.325 exposure. Skye, also revived by GH.325, is drawn deeper into questions about her own origins and what links her to the alien writing. As Hydra continues its plans—most recently testing crude Obelisk-based weapons—Simmons' cover is blown when Raina, trapped between Hydra and Skye's enigmatic father, threatens to reveal Simmons to save herself. With the timely intervention of Bobbi Morse (another S.H.I.E.L.D. plant inside Hydra), Simmons is extracted, bringing valuable intel with her.

Meanwhile, Skye is confronted by dark truths about her parentage: Ward reveals that her father is alive and possibly connected to the Obelisk, sending Skye on a desperate search. She narrowly misses finding him, only discovering evidence of his brutal nature. Raina, exposed and desperate, is now being tracked by S.H.I.E.L.D. for future leverage. As the team barely stays ahead of Hydra's relentless advancement, personal and existential stakes rise for Skye, Coulson, and the entire crew, with the secrets behind the alien writing, Skye's true nature, and her father's intentions still unresolved.
