Following the fall of S.H.I.E.L.D. to Hydra's infiltration, the remnants of the organization have splintered, with Director Coulson struggling to maintain his team and credibility. The quest to decipher the alien Diviner revealed the existence of Inhumans—people who, through Terrigenesis, gain powerful abilities. Skye underwent Terrigenesis, developing seismic powers she cannot fully control, and now finds herself alienated from her friends and grappling with guilt over the death her powers inadvertently caused. Tensions flared within S.H.I.E.L.D. as a separate faction led by Robert Gonzales, believing Coulson compromised by alien influence, staged a coup, seizing control of their base and splitting the team. Meanwhile, Fitz kept Skye's true transformation secret, while Simmons grew mistrustful, and rogue agent Ward pursued his own violent agenda.

Skye, isolated for everyone's safety, was attacked by Gonzales's agents and, losing control, gravely injured one before being spirited away by Gordon, an Inhuman with teleportation powers tied to a mysterious sanctuary called Afterlife. There, Skye meets Lincoln, who begins helping her adjust to her new identity and abilities, and learns that Raina, whose transformation was physically monstrous, is also present. Emotional confrontations between Skye and Raina reignite Skye's trauma. Jiaying, secretly Skye's long-lost mother, steps in to offer mentorship on managing Skye's abilities, asking Skye to stay and learn for a few days. Meanwhile, Jiaying denies Cal, Skye's dangerous father, the chance to see her, despite their shared concern. Back in the outside world, Coulson and Hunter go on the run from the "real S.H.I.E.L.D.," enlisting the super-powered Mike Peterson's help to evade capture. Seeing Hydra's interest in enhanced individuals, Coulson resolves to recruit Grant Ward—treacherous but resourceful—as the only person who might help them find Skye and turn the tide in the fractured struggle facing both S.H.I.E.L.D. and the Inhumans.
