Following Hydra's covert takeover and the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D., Director Coulson struggles to maintain order and purpose within a fractured organization. The team's pursuit of the alien artifact known as the Diviner reveals a larger conspiracy involving the Kree and the existence of Inhumans — people with hidden, superhuman potential. After Raina and Skye are exposed to the Terrigen crystal, both undergo profound transformations. Skye manifests seismic abilities that she struggles to control, leading to immense personal loss with the death of Agent Trip and deepening mistrust among her friends, particularly Simmons, who now views superpowered individuals as a potential threat. Fitz conceals evidence of Skye’s altered DNA to protect her, further straining team cohesion.

Amidst these personal and organizational battles, new threats and fractures emerge. Skye’s father, Cal, rallies a group of vengeful superpowered individuals to attack S.H.I.E.L.D., but his plans are thwarted when an Inhuman named Gordon intervenes and takes Cal away, hinting at the existence of a secret Inhuman community and deeper mysteries regarding their origins. Meanwhile, Hunter uncovers a splinter faction calling themselves the "real S.H.I.E.L.D.," led by Robert Gonzales, Bobbi Morse, and other former high-ranking agents. This group questions Coulson’s fitness for leadership given his obsession with alien artifacts and risky decisions. Hunter escapes their custody, while Bobbi’s loyalty to both sides is thrown into doubt.

Within S.H.I.E.L.D., Skye is removed from active duty for her own safety and sent to a secluded safehouse, pushing her further into isolation as she comes to terms with her powers. Grant Ward, now partnered with the scarred and traumatized Agent 33, plots to further his own agenda by targeting Hydra remnants and manipulating those around him, as the pair repair Agent 33’s nanomask and set their sights on Sunil Bakshi. As the internal and external threats to S.H.I.E.L.D. mount, trust is at an all-time low, and the fate of the organization — as well as Skye's place in it — remains perilously uncertain.
