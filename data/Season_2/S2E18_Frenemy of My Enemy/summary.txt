Following the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D. due to Hydra’s infiltration and the emergence of the Inhumans, Phil Coulson finds himself leading a fractured organization while internal paranoia splits S.H.I.E.L.D. in two—one side loyal to Coulson, the other to Robert Gonzales who distrusts Coulson’s secrecy and his dealings with powered individuals. Amid this chaos, Skye, revealed as Daisy Johnson and an Inhuman with seismic powers, is taken to Afterlife. Under her mother Jiaying’s guidance, she struggles to find her place between her birth family and S.H.I.E.L.D., and forms a tentative bond with her volatile father, Cal, whose past actions and emotional instability threaten to ignite further conflict. Skye is torn as she glimpses a community of Inhumans and grapples with her own powers and destiny.

As S.H.I.E.L.D. reels from internal power struggles, Coulson, now a fugitive, races to outmaneuver both Gonzales' faction and Hydra. Hydra’s leadership, obsessively hunting powered individuals, has drawn interest in Skye and others like her. To combat this, Coulson undertakes a risky alliance with Grant Ward, the team’s former traitor, offering a chance for redemption via a memory wipe in exchange for help infiltrating Hydra. With the assistance of Fitz, Hunter, and the superhuman Michael Peterson (Deathlok), Coulson orchestrates a ploy to use Sunil Bakshi as bait for Dr. List, Strucker’s right-hand man. The plan spirals when Bakshi offers Peterson to Hydra, forcing Coulson’s team to improvise and push Peterson in undercover. Meanwhile, Skye accompanies Cal on a fraught visit to his old home in Milwaukee—where their shared past comes into focus—until both are inadvertently swept into a Hydra attack, endangering Skye, Cal, and Inhuman ally Lincoln, while S.H.I.E.L.D. is inadvertently alerted to Skye’s location, deepening the crisis for all sides.

Major storylines now converge: Coulson’s faction struggles to stop Hydra’s experiments on Enhanced individuals, a divided S.H.I.E.L.D. pursues Skye, and the Inhumans reckon with Skye’s dangerous family ties. The real identities and motivations behind Coulson’s Theta Protocol and Jiaying’s leadership remain unresolved, while the fate of Skye, poised between her parents and the warring factions, becomes pivotal to the conflict brewing among humans, Inhumans, and Hydra alike.
