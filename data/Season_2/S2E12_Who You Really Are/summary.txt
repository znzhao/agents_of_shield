After the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D. due to Hydra’s infiltration, Coulson’s team is left fighting for survival, seeking to reestablish trust in a turbulent world. Their investigation into the mysterious alien artifact known as the Diviner leads them to uncover a hidden Kree city and the truth that the Diviner is a Terrigen crystal—the catalyst for Terrigenesis, which activates latent Inhuman abilities in certain humans. In the city, Skye and Raina undergo transformation following exposure to the Diviner, emerging with new powers—earthquake manipulation and a monstrous new form, respectively—while Agent Trip is killed. These events leave the team mourning and fractured, with Skye isolated, struggling with immense guilt and fear about her uncontrollable new abilities. Fitz covers up evidence of Skye’s changed DNA to protect her from growing paranoia within the team, notably Simmons, who is advocating for containment and even elimination of powered threats.

Coulson attempts to bolster S.H.I.E.L.D.’s stability by turning Hydra’s own agents against each other, but the fallout from the Kree city continues to ripple through both the team and the larger world. Meanwhile, Raina is rescued from her suicidal despair by Gordon, a teleporting Inhuman linked to Skye’s mysterious lineage and a hidden Inhuman community. As the threat posed by alien modifications grows, Lady Sif arrives on Earth with no memory, pursuing a Kree named Vin-Tak seeking evidence of Terrigenesis. With Sif’s memories restored, it is revealed that the Kree originally engineered certain humans as potential living weapons, leaving behind Diviners filled with Terrigen crystals. The Kree now see the activation of these transformations—which would alert their empire—as a major threat, and want to eliminate those affected, including Skye.

The team is split over how to handle Skye’s revelation. Sif and Vin-Tak urge containment or elimination, but Coulson defends his agent. Skye, unstable and terrified of hurting those around her, incapacitates herself to prevent her powers from going out of control. In the aftermath, relationships are strained by fear and secrecy: Fitz continues to hide truths from Simmons, Bobbi harbors secrets from Hunter, and May steps in as Skye’s protector. The existence of other transformed humans, the threat of additional Diviners, and Skye’s growing powers remain unresolved, leaving S.H.I.E.L.D. facing both external alien dangers and internal conflict over trust, humanity, and control.
