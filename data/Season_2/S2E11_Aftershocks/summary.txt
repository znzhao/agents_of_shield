Following the catastrophic collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D. and the exposure of Hydra’s infiltration, Coulson’s team has fought to survive amidst betrayals, shifting loyalties, and new threats tied to an alien artifact called the Diviner. The team's relentless pursuit of answers led to the discovery of a hidden Kree city, where the object’s true power—Terrigenesis—altered or killed those exposed. Skye and Raina survived the transformation, but Trip was killed; Skye emerged with seismic powers and Raina was physically transformed. The fallout left the team fractured, mourning Trip, and grappling with the alien forces now in their midst.

Skye, isolated and under observation, struggles with her guilt and fear over her new abilities. The rest of the team processes their grief and blames, with divisions surfacing, particularly as Mack lashes out, feeling responsible due to his own possession in the alien city. Coulson attempts to reassert leadership by using Hydra agent Bakshi against rival Hydra leaders, successfully sowing chaos among their ranks. Meanwhile, Simmons, increasingly alarmed by the alien changes, investigates Raina—now violently monstrous—and worries about an epidemic. Fitz discovers Skye’s DNA is as drastically changed as Raina's, but, protecting her, covers up the evidence from Simmons and May.

Elsewhere, Raina, tormented by her grotesque transformation and rejected by Cal, attempts suicide but is intercepted by a mysterious group of Inhumans. Gordon, rescued and mentored decades earlier by Skye’s mother Jiaying, now emerges to guide Raina, hinting at a larger Inhuman community ready to reveal itself. Skye’s lineage and the extent of her powers remain a looming mystery, while S.H.I.E.L.D. is left reeling, fractured by loss, new dangers, and the growing divide between science and the supernatural.
