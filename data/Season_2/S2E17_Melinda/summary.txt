After the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D. due to Hydra’s infiltration, Phil Coulson tries to hold together a fractured organization. Alien artifacts lead to the revelation of the Inhumans—a hidden race of people with potentially dangerous powers awakened through Terrigenesis. Skye, one of Coulson’s closest allies, undergoes her own transformation and gains seismic powers that leave her alienated and wracked with guilt after she unintentionally injures another agent. Amid growing paranoia about powered individuals and Coulson’s secrecy, internal divisions erupt: a separate S.H.I.E.L.D. faction led by Robert Gonzales seizes control of their primary base, believing Coulson’s judgment is compromised.

As Coulson and a shrinking band of allies go underground, Skye is taken in by the Inhumans and begins training under Jiaying, who is secretly her mother. Jiaying guides Skye, teaching her to control her abilities, and ultimately reveals their true relationship while counseling her to keep it secret to avoid destabilizing Afterlife’s delicate hierarchy. Meanwhile, flashbacks reveal the trauma that shaped Melinda May: years earlier, May was forced to kill a young Inhuman girl in Bahrain to prevent a catastrophe, an event that earned her the feared nickname "The Cavalry" and emotionally estranged her from her husband and colleagues. This incident is revealed to connect to current events, as Jiaying had been monitoring Inhuman transgressions for years.

Within S.H.I.E.L.D., trust continues to erode. May, now woven into Gonzales’s command circle, investigates Coulson’s secretive "Theta Protocol" and his contacts with powered individuals. Fitz, remaining loyal to Coulson, escapes with vital data and contacts his fugitive friends as the rest of the team grows increasingly suspicious. May learns how Coulson has been diverting resources for unknown purposes and that he may be seeking to establish a haven or training ground for powered people. As rival factions maneuver, the fates of S.H.I.E.L.D., the Inhumans, and Skye’s place between both worlds hang in precarious balance.
