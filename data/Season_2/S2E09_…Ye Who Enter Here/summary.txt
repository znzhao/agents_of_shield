Following the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D., Coulson’s team has been hunted by Hydra while grappling with paranoia, fractured trust, and lingering questions about alien artifacts. The mysterious Obelisk, or Diviner, is revealed as a deadly device tied to alien lineage, capable of killing most but judging some as 'worthy.' Coulson’s compulsive alien carvings are discovered to be blueprints for a hidden city, connected to Skye’s origins and an ancient alien race—the Kree. As Hydra’s Dr. Whitehall, really the ageless Dr. Reinhardt, seeks to access the city for his own power, Skye’s father Cal, driven by affection and madness, allies with Hydra to find his daughter.

Personal stakes intensify: Grant Ward severs all ties to his family in a shocking act of violence, re-positioning himself as a wild card between both S.H.I.E.L.D. and Hydra. Fitz struggles with brain damage but gradually regains confidence with Mack’s support, and the group's unity is continually challenged by betrayals, secrets, and the threat of infiltration. Simmons and Morse uncover Whitehall's dark history, while Raina's mysterious resilience links her, like Skye, to the Obelisk’s secrets. S.H.I.E.L.D. races Hydra to a hidden city beneath Puerto Rico, fearing what opening it might unleash.

In Puerto Rico, Mack is possessed after making contact with alien technology in ancient ruins, violently attacking his teammates and ultimately vanishing into a deep chasm. Meanwhile, Skye, May, Hunter, and Trip protect Raina from Hydra, learning that the Obelisk is Kree in origin and that only certain people can survive the temple within the city. As Whitehall issues a dire ultimatum—hand over Raina or be destroyed—S.H.I.E.L.D. is left fractured, under siege, and bracing for a life-or-death race into the unknown depths below.
