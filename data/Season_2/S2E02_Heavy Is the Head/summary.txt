The world of S.H.I.E.L.D. has been fundamentally shaken by Hydra’s infiltration, leading to S.H.I.E.L.D.’s collapse and deep internal mistrust. Phil Coulson, appointed Director by Nick Fury, has rallied a core team—including May, Skye, Fitz, Simmons, and Trip—to survive in the shadows and resist Hydra’s power grabs. They have faced betrayals like Grant Ward’s duplicity and Fitz’s near-fatal injuries, which left him mentally impaired and isolated, especially after Simmons’ unexplained departure. The team’s unity remains tested by constant losses and the weight of carrying on S.H.I.E.L.D.’s legacy with scant resources.

Recently, Coulson’s team attempted to secure a powerful artifact known as the Obelisk, which Hydra and the enigmatic Daniel Whitehall covet. The operation results in disaster: mercenaries Hartley and Idaho are killed by Hydra agent Carl Creel, who absconds with the artifact. Lance Hunter, surviving but traumatized, is manipulated by General Talbot but ultimately stays loyal to the team after demanding Hartley be given a proper burial. Fitz, struggling with his mental decline, forms a fragile new partnership with Mack while continuing to aid the team technologically. Meanwhile, Raina reappears and steals the Obelisk, delivering it to Skye’s mysterious father and revealing—via her immunity to the artifact’s deadly effects—hints of a deeper connection to Skye’s heritage.

Coulson himself is under significant strain. He experiences unexplained episodes where he compulsively carves strange runes linked to the Obelisk’s markings, a secret May helps him conceal from the team. The team finally manages to stop Creel using Fitz’s improvised device, but Hydra’s threat and Whitehall’s machinations are ongoing, with the fate of the Obelisk still unsettled and Skye’s parentage shrouded in mystery. Amid pervasive danger and internal fractures, the rebuilt S.H.I.E.L.D. struggles to hold together as it races to learn the true significance of the Obelisk, Skye’s origins, and Coulson’s unraveling mind.
