In the aftermath of S.H.I.E.L.D.'s collapse due to Hydra's infiltration, Coulson leads his small, fractured team underground as they work to preserve what remains of their organization. The team struggles with major trust issues following Ward's betrayal and Fitz's traumatic brain injury, which leaves him socially withdrawn and mentally impaired, relying heavily on Mack for support. Simmons operates undercover within Hydra, spying for S.H.I.E.L.D. but living in constant danger of exposure. Hydra, led by Daniel Whitehall and his agent Bakshi, continues their campaign of recruitment and brainwashing, seeking enhanced individuals and powerful artifacts, including the alien Obelisk, closely tied to Skye’s mysterious origins and Coulson's own compulsions.

As S.H.I.E.L.D. stays on the move, they face escalating threats from Hydra's schemes. Simmons is nearly exposed during a Hydra operation to retrieve the superhuman Donnie Gill, showcasing both Hydra's fearsome brainwashing methods and the increasing stakes. Ward, now imprisoned by Coulson, becomes a tense source of intelligence and a painful reminder of the team's fractures, especially for Fitz. Meanwhile, Skye matures as an agent, taking decisive, sometimes lethal action, but continues to wrestle with her heritage and Coulson’s deteriorating mental state, marked by his obsessive carving of alien runes.

The latest crisis involves a centuries-old painting miraculously preserved in a church fire, bearing the same mysterious runes. Coulson and May go undercover to recover it, only to be ensnared in a Hydra plot involving Bakshi's operatives and bio-mimetic disguises. May is captured and fought by her doppelganger, while Coulson narrowly escapes Bakshi. Simultaneously, a Hydra virus nearly destroys the S.H.I.E.L.D. plane, only stopped at the last minute thanks to Fitz’s guidance. Analysis of the painting reveals the runes are recent, indicating another individual afflicted like Coulson. Shaken, Coulson tasks May with the grim order to kill him if he succumbs to madness. The team remains high-functioning under pressure but fragile, with Hydra ever-adapting and S.H.I.E.L.D.'s survival uncertain.
