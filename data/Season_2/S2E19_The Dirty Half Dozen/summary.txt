In the aftermath of S.H.I.E.L.D.'s collapse and Hydra's secretive infiltration, leadership fractures between the pragmatic but secretive Phil Coulson and the cautious Robert Gonzales. The organization is torn between distrust and necessity, especially as Coulson's secretive operations and connections to powered individuals spark anxiety among traditional agents. Skye's transformation into Daisy Johnson, an Inhuman with newly awakened seismic powers, leads her to Afterlife, a hidden Inhuman sanctuary. There, she finds guidance from her mother Jiaying and is reluctantly drawn into the emotional orbit of her unstable father, Cal. Emotional tensions run high as Cal inadvertently reveals to the Inhuman community the true nature of Skye's family, complicating her loyalties further. Meanwhile, Hydra intensifies its abduction and experimentation on Gifteds, taking both S.H.I.E.L.D. agent Deathlok (Mike Peterson) and Inhuman Lincoln prisoner.

Coulson manipulates events to force an alliance with Gonzales, persuading him to sanction a joint mission against Hydra's Arctic facility in exchange for access to Fury's toolbox of secrets. The original fractured "family" of S.H.I.E.L.D. – Coulson, May, Fitz, Simmons, Skye, and even the treacherous Ward – is reunited out of necessity. Old wounds fester, especially around Ward's betrayals, and trust remains frayed. Raina's prophetic visions spur Skye to risk her own safety, coordinating via the teleporter Gordon to join the S.H.I.E.L.D. strike mission in rescuing Lincoln and Peterson. The raid escalates dangerously: Hydra is tipped off, the facility comes under attack, and Dr. List escapes. Skye demonstrates her extraordinary abilities by rescuing Lincoln from death, while Simmons' attempt to assassinate Ward with a splinter bomb accidentally kills their Hydra asset, Bakshi. Ward, disillusioned by Simmons’ ruthlessness, departs, and Coulson secretly downloads Hydra data, keeping secrets even from his allies.

Amidst these battles, key mysteries and questions remain unresolved: the true agenda of Coulson's Theta Protocol, Jiaying's intentions as Inhuman leader, and the fate of powered individuals as war brews on all fronts. The precarious alliance within S.H.I.E.L.D. is held together only by common enemies, tensions around Ward’s presence remain raw, and Skye is left tangled between her two families and struggling to reconcile her place among humans and Inhumans. Hydra, battered but unbroken, retains dangerous knowledge about Gifteds, and the next stage of conflict looms.
