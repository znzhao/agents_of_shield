Agent Phil Coulson, having been mysteriously resurrected after the Battle of New York, leads a S.H.I.E.L.D. team consisting of Melinda May, Grant Ward, Leo Fitz, Jemma Simmons, and the skilled hacker Skye. The team confronts global threats stemming from advanced technology and super-powered individuals, many orchestrated by the shadowy Centipede group led by the mysterious Clairvoyant. Skye’s search for her own origins reveals she is a "0-8-4"—someone of unknown and potentially dangerous provenance—prompting questions about her past and the agency’s willingness to protect her at great cost. Coulson himself struggles with revelations about his own resurrection via secretive and traumatic procedures, deepening his doubts about the organization’s ethics and leadership.

The team’s battle escalates with the involvement of the ruthless industrialist Ian Quinn, culminating in an undercover mission in Italy where Skye is gravely wounded after discovering Quinn’s alliance with project Centipede and the transformed, cybernetic Mike Peterson (now codenamed Deathlok). As Skye’s life hangs by a thread, the agents fracture with grief and blame, but Coulson’s determination prevails. Refusing to let Skye die, Coulson orchestrates a desperate mission to a covert facility known as the Guest House, uncovering the existence of a secret serum (GH 325) responsible for his resurrection—a substance sourced from a mysterious, blue-skinned alien corpse. Despite Coulson’s warnings, Simmons uses the serum to save Skye’s life, stabilizing her condition. The agents John Garrett and Antoine Triplett aid the operation, claiming custody of Quinn for further interrogation. Coulson is burdened by what he has seen and what was risked for Skye’s survival, as the true nature and dangers of the GH serum—and those who would seek it, including the Clairvoyant—remain ominously unresolved.
