A young father, Michael Peterson, unexpectedly demonstrates superhuman abilities when an explosion strikes a building, using his powers to save a trapped woman while his son Ace and a bystander watch. His heroic act is caught on video by Skye, an underground hacktivist who broadcasts about powerful individuals and exposes secret organizations like S.H.I.E.L.D. Meanwhile, S.H.I.E.L.D. operative Grant Ward foils a theft in Paris, only to be recalled by Agent Maria Hill and surprised by the return of Phil Coulson, believed to be dead after the battle involving the Avengers. Coulson, whose resurrection is shrouded in secrecy, begins assembling a specialist team for S.H.I.E.L.D., including the reluctant Agent May and the science duo Fitz and Simmons.

As Skye tracks down Peterson and tries to warn him about both S.H.I.E.L.D. and the shadowy groups that would exploit or destroy him, the agency closes in, ultimately bringing Skye into custody. They learn Peterson's powers stem from an experimental device known as Centipede, blending Extremis, gamma radiation, and super soldier serum—recalling previous catastrophes. The Centipede’s side effects make its recipients volatile and unstable. As Peterson spirals—attacking his boss and clashing with his mysterious benefactors—he takes Skye hostage, desperate to protect himself and his son. In a tense showdown, Coulson’s team subdues Peterson using nonlethal force, sparing his life, and hinting at both deeper dangers behind Centipede and the emerging cohesion of Coulson’s new team. The mystery of Coulson’s resurrection and the threat of Centipede remain unsolved, while Skye’s status as a potential new recruit suggests shifting allegiances within the group.
