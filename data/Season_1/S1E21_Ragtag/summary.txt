Agent Phil Coulson’s S.H.I.E.L.D. team—consisting of May, Ward, Fitz, Simmons, Skye, and later Triplett—has been swept up in a clandestine war against the Hydra-controlled Centipede project, which has weaponized both super-soldier serum and alien technology. As the team battles external threats, internal trust fractures with the revelation that Coulson’s own resurrection ties to an alien-derived serum known as GH.325, and secrets fester regarding Skye’s mysterious background. The agency is shattered when S.H.I.E.L.D. is revealed to be compromised at the highest levels by Hydra led by the Clairvoyant, now unmasked as John Garrett. Ward, long believed loyal, is revealed as Garrett’s double agent, betraying and endangering his teammates, culminating in their becoming fugitives and the group’s emotional and operational unraveling.

Following the Hydra uprising, the fractured team regroups under immense pressure, seeking both vengeance and answers. Skye and May, haunted by guilt and suspicion, draw closer, while Fitz struggles to accept Ward’s betrayal. With the leadership mantle thrust more heavily on Coulson, the team tracks the Hydra conspiracy through high-tech subterfuge and old spycraft, tracing Cybertek—the company behind Deathlok—back to its roots. Revelations mount: Centipede’s project started decades prior, with Garrett himself as the first Deathlok subject, now desperate to prolong his life with the serum that once saved Coulson and Skye. Garrett manipulates Ward through a toxic mentor-mentee relationship, shaping him into a weapon but leaving deep emotional scars. Skye’s past takes on new significance as Raina exposes her as an orphan touched by violence and sought after by monsters—raising the possibility of an extraordinary heritage.

The team’s latest gambit leads them to Cuba, where they attempt to outmaneuver Garrett, Ward, and Hydra. Through acts of bravery and sacrifice, including daring operations at Cybertek and infiltration of Hydra's base, they learn just how outmatched they are by Hydra’s resources and brutality. Ward’s final test of loyalty culminates in him seemingly disposing of Fitz and Simmons at Garrett’s command, while Garrett himself, on the verge of death, is injected with a blend of alien serum and Centipede formula, emerging with newfound vigor and possible unpredictability. Coulson’s remaining team is cornered and outgunned by Hydra’s Centipede-enhanced soldiers, setting the stage for a final confrontation amid deepening uncertainty and loss.
