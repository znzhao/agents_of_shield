After Phil Coulson’s mysterious resurrection, he is assigned to lead a new team for S.H.I.E.L.D., comprised of May, Ward, Fitz, Simmons, and Skye. Their initial missions unravel a deep conspiracy involving a group called Centipede and its enigmatic leader, the Clairvoyant, and also draw them into Skye’s hidden past involving an alien serum known as GH-325. Over time, trust within the team is tested by secrets—like May's surveillance on Coulson’s recovery and Skye’s hacking prowess—while the threat of Hydra grows more imminent.

The revelation that the Clairvoyant is actually John Garrett, a high-ranking S.H.I.E.L.D. agent and leader within Hydra, shatters the team. Ward, previously trusted, is exposed as Garrett’s loyal mole—ultimately assassinating agents and manipulating his teammates. S.H.I.E.L.D. collapses under Hydra’s assault, forcing Coulson’s team on the run. They find sanctuary at the hidden Providence base, led by Eric Koenig. There, paranoia lingers as Coulson’s leadership continues despite internal doubts, and Ward, still undercover, is tasked with abducting Skye for Hydra. Meanwhile, Raina joins Garrett in researching the alien-derived serum, and the team realizes the scale of Hydra’s infiltration as they lose allies and vital resources.

Currently, following a breakout at the Fridge which unleashes dangerous prisoners and weaponized tech, Coulson pursues Marcus Daniels—a Darkforce-powered killer obsessed with Coulson’s former love Audrey. The team neutralizes Daniels in Portland, but at Providence, Koenig’s trust tests leave Ward exposed. After murdering Koenig to guard his identity, Ward manipulates Skye into leaving the base with him, forcing her into a perilous situation as she uncovers Ward’s Hydra allegiance. May, feeling betrayed and mistrusted, leaves the team. As Skye is taken hostage on the Bus and the rest of the team returns to find Koenig dead, trust is shattered, Hydra’s threat is more perilous than ever, and S.H.I.E.L.D.’s future hangs by a thread.
