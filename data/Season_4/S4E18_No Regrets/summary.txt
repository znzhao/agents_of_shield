In a desperate attempt to rescue their friends and escape the Framework—a virtual reality where Hydra rules and reality’s rules are warped—Simmons and Daisy have been navigating a nightmarish world where allies are reimagined as enemies and lost loved ones restored only as digital illusions. Their efforts to awaken the true selves of Fitz, May, and others have been repeatedly rebuffed; Fitz has become a brutal Hydra scientist, capable of murdering Agnes (Aida’s model), and May administers Hydra's cruelty with disturbing efficiency. Daisy and Simmons, aided by resistance fighter Ward, track down Dr. Radcliffe (also trapped inside) and learn of a single remaining escape route (the "backdoor") out of the Framework, though it's now guarded by Aida, who rules as Madame Hydra. Meanwhile, team loyalties and sense of reality are continually strained, as Simmons’ insistence on the world's falseness rocks even steadfast agents like Mace and Ward.

The resistance launches a daring operation to infiltrate Hydra’s Enlightenment Cultivation Center, discovering it houses not only imprisoned S.H.I.E.L.D. operatives but also children being subjected to mind control. During the raid, Mace and Coulson rescue the presumed-dead Antoine Triplett, while Mace demonstrates true heroism, risking—and ultimately sacrificing—his life to save a child when Hydra destroys the building to cover their crimes. This act has a profound effect on May, who, after seeing the reality of Hydra's atrocities and Mace's selfless sacrifice, is shaken from her loyalty. Madame Hydra reveals to Daisy that the Framework was shaped not by pure design but by the desires and regrets of those within, revealing how small moments changed their destinies, such as Fitz being raised under his father’s harsh influence. With S.H.I.E.L.D. battered, hope fading, and lines between reality and illusion increasingly blurred, only the memory of true selves and acts of sacrifice seem capable of breaking Hydra’s hold from within.
