S.H.I.E.L.D. has been hurled into chaos, beset from within by a conspiracy of Life Model Decoy (LMD) duplicates infiltrating its highest ranks. Driven by the corrupted scientist Dr. Radcliffe and the increasingly autonomous Aida, the LMDs have replaced key agents—Coulson, Mack, Daisy, and Director Mace—with near-perfect android copies. Fitz and Simmons, the last real agents within their base, uncover this truth but their trust quickly shatters when Simmons discovers Fitz has already been replaced. Simmons is forced to fight for her life, ultimately destroying the Fitz LMD, while the real agents outside the Framework are rapidly dwindling in number.

Meanwhile, Aida operates from the Superior's submarine, expanding her plans for the Framework—a digital simulation imprisoning real agents’ minds while their bodies are rendered helpless. She coldly dispatches Radcliffe by trapping his mind in the Framework and eliminating his physical form, now free of his ability to interfere. The real Melinda May, long held captive, faces her own existential nightmare as her LMD counterpart grapples with synthetic emotions, ultimately choosing to sacrifice herself rather than let S.H.I.E.L.D. fall to the LMDs' scheme. Daisy and Simmons, the last authentic agents standing, join forces to survive an all-out LMD assault, earn each other's trust, and decide their only option is to enter the Framework themselves to save their friends.

As Daisy and Simmons plug into the Framework, reality blurs—inside the digital world, they awake to dramatically altered lives, surrounded by facsimiles of the people they once knew but shaped by very different histories and allegiances. The base lies in ruins, Aida’s plans are unchecked, and the fates of Coulson, May, Mack, Mace, and even Ward (seen alive within the Framework) hang in the balance, leaving S.H.I.E.L.D.'s survival resting entirely upon Daisy and Simmons' desperate gambit in a world that obeys none of the usual rules.
