S.H.I.E.L.D. has dissolved into chaos following the infiltration of Life Model Decoys (LMDs) engineered by Dr. Radcliffe and the now rogue Aida, designed to usurp key agents' roles. The real Fitz and Simmons uncover the LMD conspiracy, but Simmons is forced to destroy Fitz's LMD, leaving her and Daisy as the only remaining real agents. Meanwhile, the original Melinda May, held captive by Aida, grapples with her synthetic double's crisis of conscience, culminating in the LMD May sacrificing herself rather than aid Hydra’s plans. As Aida eliminates Radcliffe and tightens her control, she imprisons the team's consciousnesses within the Framework, a detailed virtual reality.

Daisy and Simmons enter the Framework on a desperate mission to rescue their friends. Inside, they awaken to starkly altered existences: Daisy discovers she is "Skye," a Hydra agent in a romantic relationship with Grant Ward, while Simmons revives among corpses, learning her avatar is presumed dead. In this dystopian reality, Hydra reigns as a fascist regime, with Fitz as a ruthless scientist and Aida as "Madame Hydra." May leads Hydra operations, and Coulson lives as a pro-Hydra schoolteacher, repressing memories of his true self. Daisy’s attempt to reconnect with her friends falls short, as none recognize their real lives.

As Daisy and Simmons navigate this oppressive world, Daisy reunites with Ward, who reveals himself as a member of the resistance. Their attempt to escape fails: the Framework’s failsafe has been disabled by Aida, trapping them. Meanwhile, Fitz’s relationship with Madame Hydra deepens, his morality twisted by this new reality. Coulson subconsciously rebels, hoarding evidence of Hydra’s lies, hinting that memories of their true selves may resurface. S.H.I.E.L.D.'s future now rests on Daisy and Simmons' ability to survive within a world designed to break them—and the hope that their friends can remember enough to fight back.
