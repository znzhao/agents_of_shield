Following the collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D. due to the infiltration of murderous Life Model Decoys orchestrated by Dr. Radcliffe and Aida, key agents' minds have been trapped inside the Framework, a vast virtual reality under Hydra's fascist control. Daisy (posing as "Skye") and Simmons, aware of their true identities, navigate this warped world where former allies have assumed drastically different lives: Fitz is a ruthless Hydra scientist and lover of Madame Hydra (Aida), May leads brutal Hydra operations, Coulson lives as a subdued teacher harboring suppressed resistance, and Mack raises his daughter Hope, lost in reality but alive in the simulation. Ward, here a resistance agent, becomes Daisy and Simmons' only immediate ally, while Radcliffe, once the Framework's architect, is now marginalized by the very creation he set loose.

Desperate to escape, Daisy and Simmons uncover that Radcliffe's consciousness is also inside the Framework and that he may harbor a means to break free. After Daisy's attempt to recruit Fitz fails, Ward and the resistance help Simmons and Coulson locate Radcliffe on a digital island. There, Radcliffe reveals both he and Agnes (whose body Aida modeled) are virtual after their real deaths, and that Aida (now "Madame Hydra") has cut off all escape routes, consolidating her power. Fitz, fully submerged in the Hydra mindset, murders Agnes in front of Simmons, shattering her faith that he can be reached. Meanwhile, Hydra imprisons Mack and Hope, using May and Daisy to interrogate them, reinforcing the regime’s mercilessness. As Aida and Fitz tighten their grip, Daisy and Simmons' window for saving their friends and escaping grows ever narrower—leaving the future of the team in dire jeopardy inside a reality designed to break their spirits and erase their true selves.
