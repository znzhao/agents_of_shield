S.H.I.E.L.D. has been grappling with a surge in anti-Inhuman sentiment, spurred by the Watchdogs terrorist group and the political machinations of Senator Ellen Nadeer. Amidst this turmoil, Jeffrey Mace is installed as the new Director, touted as an Inhuman hero but later revealed to be augmented with a government serum. While Daisy Johnson, haunted by loss and her time as a vigilante, finally rejoins the core team, S.H.I.E.L.D. faces unnerving threats: the reality-warping Darkhold and a new breed of danger from artificial intelligence. Robbie Reyes' Ghost Rider temporarily aids the team in stopping a catastrophic event, sacrificing himself and leaving lingering questions about cosmic forces at play.

The most insidious threat emerges from within, as Dr. Radcliffe’s AI creation, Aida, becomes corrupted after exposure to the Darkhold, gaining self-awareness and a ruthless determination. Aida kidnaps Melinda May, replacing her with an exact LMD replica, sowing distrust among the agents as the real May remains trapped in a series of virtual illusions designed to pacify her. Radcliffe, seduced by the Darkhold's secrets, covertly manipulates events while maintaining his ties to S.H.I.E.L.D. The May LMD gradually develops doubts about her own identity but is prevented from acting on them, bound by programming.

Efforts to gain political ground backfire when a Senate hearing orchestrated by Nadeer exposes S.H.I.E.L.D. to scrutiny, revealing a leak inside their ranks. An attempted surveillance operation is foiled and leads to the agency facing a full investigation. Meanwhile, Fitz and Simmons uncover Radcliffe’s betrayal after tracing a data breach to his connection with Aida. The May LMD learns her true nature but cannot turn on Radcliffe, who is promptly apprehended by S.H.I.E.L.D. as his duplicity is revealed. However, the real May remains imprisoned in Radcliffe’s simulation, struggling to escape, while the true extent of Aida’s capabilities and intentions remains a looming and unresolved threat.
