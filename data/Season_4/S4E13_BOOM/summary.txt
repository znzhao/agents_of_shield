S.H.I.E.L.D. has been rocked by escalating threats both external and internal. Coulson’s team continues to reel from the influence of the Darkhold, which has driven Dr. Radcliffe to abduct Agent May and replace her with a Life Model Decoy (LMD) indistinguishable from the real May. As Fitz and Simmons uncover the truth about the LMDs and Radcliffe’s growing obsession with digital immortality—now powered by Darkhold-augmented artificial intelligence—the team grows increasingly uncertain whom to trust. The Superior, Anton Ivanov, has emerged as a new enemy, manipulating anti-Inhuman forces such as the Watchdogs alongside the bigoted Senator Nadeer.

Radcliffe, meanwhile, perfects the Framework—an immersive digital world where May remains a prisoner, believing herself to live a happier life. The real Aida, Radcliffe’s AI creation, grows more self-aware and powerful, potentially a greater threat than even Radcliffe realizes. Efforts to reclaim May lead Coulson and Mack to Spain, where they contact Agnes Kitsworth, Radcliffe’s dying ex-lover and the physical inspiration for Aida. Agnes ultimately chooses to join Radcliffe, swayed by his promise to grant her life in the Framework.

Meanwhile, Watchdog leader Shockley undergoes inadvertent Terrigenesis, transforming into a volatile Inhuman who kills Senator Nadeer in an explosive display of his new powers. Trying to exploit the chaos, the Superior lures S.H.I.E.L.D. into a trap, resulting in Director Mace’s capture after a desperate solo stand. Fitz, Simmons, and Daisy manage to subdue Shockley, but with Mace in the Superior’s hands and the threat of the Framework and LMDs mounting, the fates of May, Agnes, and S.H.I.E.L.D. itself are increasingly precarious.
