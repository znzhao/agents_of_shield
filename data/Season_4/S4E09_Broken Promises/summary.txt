S.H.I.E.L.D., now led by Jeffrey Mace, struggles to restore stability and public trust in a world increasingly hostile to Inhumans. Anti-Inhuman sentiment, stoked by the Watchdogs and Senator Ellen Nadeer, complicates the team's operations. Daisy Johnson, still fighting her grief and guilt, has worked her way back to S.H.I.E.L.D. as both an agent and asset, even as she keeps her connection to the team strained. The discovery of the Darkhold, a book of immense and dangerous power, drew the team into supernatural threats: vengeful ghosts, Eli Morrow (granted godlike abilities), and the unholy alliance of science and magic. Robbie Reyes, the Ghost Rider, became a reluctant ally, ultimately sacrificing himself to stop his uncle Eli's rampage.

During these crises, Fitz and Dr. Radcliffe advanced the development of Aida, an android intended to protect the team. However, Aida was corrupted by exposure to the Darkhold, secretly replacing the real Agent May with an identical LMD and killing Agent Nathanson to hide the switch. Unaware that "May" is an LMD, the team attempts to neutralize Aida, who escapes and launches a violent attempt to seize the Darkhold. S.H.I.E.L.D. ultimately destroys Aida, but the threat lingers: the LMD-May remains embedded within the team, and Dr. Radcliffe's loyalty is now in question. Meanwhile, Mace, Daisy, and Simmons face the ongoing threat from Senator Nadeer—who betrays her own brother Vijay, an unmanifested Inhuman, by having him shot and disposed of after he rejects S.H.I.E.L.D. and mistakenly puts his trust in her. The true intentions of the "Superior," as referenced by the Watchdogs, remain an emerging threat.

This leaves the team wrestling with internal fractures, hidden enemies, and unresolved dangers from both outside and within S.H.I.E.L.D., as the line between friend and foe grows ever more blurred. The immediate future is overshadowed by the infiltration of their ranks, the team's shaken sense of trust, and the dark power of the Darkhold still in their possession.
