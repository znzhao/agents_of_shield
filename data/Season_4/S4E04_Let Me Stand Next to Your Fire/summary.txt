After the fall of Hive, S.H.I.E.L.D. has emerged from the shadows but faces intense government oversight under its new Inhuman Director, Jeffrey Mace. Phil Coulson is no longer the Director and works as a field agent, grappling with shifting team dynamics. Daisy Johnson, distancing herself from her friends due to guilt and a drive for justice, operates as the solo vigilante Quake, hunting down Watchdog operatives and risking her physical health as her powers take a damaging toll on her. Daisy's path brings her into direct conflict with the Watchdogs, a domestic terrorist group targeting Inhumans with increasing effectiveness, now armed with S.H.I.E.L.D.'s own Inhuman asset database after a mysterious data breach.

Meanwhile, the supernatural thread intensifies as ghostly entities—victims of a failed experiment at Momentum Labs involving a dangerous artifact called the Darkhold—haunt the city. The enigmatic Ghost Rider, Robbie Reyes, with infernal powers, is drawn into the conflict when family ties to both the Momentum incident and Eli Morrow, his imprisoned uncle and a key engineer, come to light. Coulson, Mack, and Robbie form an uneasy alliance to uncover the truth behind Momentum and the Darkhold, learning that Lucy Bauer, one of the ghosts, is seeking the book that started it all. Dr. Radcliffe and Fitz secretly push the boundaries of artificial intelligence with the Life Model Decoy Aida, who successfully passes as human while helping to heal Agent May, though her true nature remains hidden from most of the team.

Daisy reconnects with Simmons for help in tracking compromised Inhumans, and they discover that James—one of their former allies still wracked with self-loathing after his time under Hive's sway—has betrayed other Inhumans to the Watchdogs. In a dramatic confrontation at James' workplace, Ghost Rider intervenes to save Daisy and Simmons. James is subdued, but with the Watchdogs still at large, May's fragile recovery, and the unresolved threat of the Darkhold and the ghosts, the future remains fraught with danger. Relationships are strained: Daisy remains an outcast, Fitz and Simmons' hopes for normalcy are threatened by S.H.I.E.L.D.'s secrets, and the team's alliances—both internal and with supernatural forces—are as unstable as ever.
