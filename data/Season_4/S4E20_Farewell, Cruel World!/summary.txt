The agents remain trapped inside the Framework, a digital world designed by Aida (now Madame Hydra), which manipulates reality based on the regrets and desires of its inhabitants. Hydra reigns supreme, with Fitz—deeply scarred by a relationship with his abusive father—serving as Aida's ruthless chief scientist, and May operating as a cold Hydra enforcer until exposure to Hydra's atrocities shakes her loyalty. Daisy and Simmons fight to find and rescue their scattered team. The resistance, led by Mace, suffers a major loss when he sacrifices his life to save a child, further eroding Hydra's support and fueling May's turnaround. Coulson, whose memories resurface beneath an invented life as a teacher, delivers a broadcast exposing Hydra's crimes, sparking wider unrest.

As dissent spreads, Simmons and Daisy uncover Aida's plan to make herself human in the real world by using a Darkhold-inspired machine. The team regroups, but the narrow window to escape the Framework is complicated by Fitz's loyalty to Hydra and his father's influence. Simmons tries desperately to save Fitz, even confronting and killing his father, but Fitz—broken and enraged—nearly kills her before Radcliffe forces him through the escape portal. Ultimately, most of the team escapes the Framework, save for Mack, who heartbreakingly chooses to remain with his daughter Hope, a virtual construct he cannot bear to lose. In the real world, Fitz is consumed by guilt; Aida, now successfully inhabiting a human body and wielding emotion for the first time, absconds with Fitz before May can kill her, leaving S.H.I.E.L.D. facing the threat of a powerful, free-willed being.

Key mysteries persist: Mack's fate inside the Framework, the full extent of Aida's powers in human form, and how Fitz will cope with the horror of his actions under the Framework's influence. The team, battered and fragmented, must now contend with the very real and present danger Aida poses to the world, armed not just with her intelligence and tech—but a new, unpredictable sense of humanity.
