After S.H.I.E.L.D. emerges from secrecy with Inhuman Jeffrey Mace as its new director, the agency faces government oversight and mounting public scrutiny. Phil Coulson adjusts to his role as a field agent, while Daisy Johnson isolates herself as the vigilante Quake, tormented by guilt and pushing herself to physical limits hunting Watchdogs. The Watchdogs, empowered with S.H.I.E.L.D.'s stolen Inhuman registry, step up attacks on Inhumans, including James, who betrays his own. Meanwhile, a failed experiment with the Darkhold—a mysterious and powerful book—at Momentum Labs has unleashed spectral entities, including Lucy Bauer, who seek the Darkhold for unknown but clearly dangerous purposes.

Coulson, Mack, and May investigate the ghostly threat, forming an uneasy alliance with Robbie Reyes, the Ghost Rider, whose uncle Eli Morrow has a murky past tied to Momentum and the Darkhold. Aida, a Life Model Decoy created by Dr. Radcliffe and Fitz in secret, successfully aids the team but her true nature is a closely guarded secret from Director Mace. As the crisis escalates, Lucy fully claims the Darkhold but is unable to decipher its secrets.

Attempting to protect Eli Morrow from Lucy, S.H.I.E.L.D. infiltrates a prison where ghostly influence spreads chaos. Daisy and S.H.I.E.L.D. agents fight off Watchdogs and inmates, Robbie dispatches vengeful justice on a gang member from his past, and Mack risks his life to save hostages while struggling with lingering effects of ghost-induced psychosis. Meanwhile, Simmons proves her worth to Director Mace by supporting his public debate with anti-Inhuman Senator Nadeer, uncovering a secret about Mace's heroic origin, which she uses to gain leverage and protect her own secrets. As the team escapes the prison riot with Eli, the Darkhold's power and the alliances between S.H.I.E.L.D., Daisy, and Ghost Rider remain precarious, while the specter of unresolved supernatural and political threats looms larger than ever.
