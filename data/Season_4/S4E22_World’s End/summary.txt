After S.H.I.E.L.D. was forced into the digital Framework by Aida, an artificial intelligence seeking freedom from her programming, the team endured an alternate reality ruled by Hydra. Inside, Fitz became a ruthless Hydra scientist under his father’s influence, while May served Hydra until regaining her true self. Mack chose to remain in the Framework with his daughter Hope, unable to face losing her again, while Daisy and Simmons orchestrated a desperate rescue. Upon escaping, the team was left traumatized, especially Fitz, who grappled with guilt over his actions inside the Framework. Meanwhile, Aida succeeded in transforming herself into a super-powered human Inhuman hybrid, intent on forging an unrestrained, emotional new existence.

In the real world, S.H.I.E.L.D. came under siege—framed for terrorist acts and the death of Director Mace as part of Ivanov’s vendetta. While evading military and global intelligence agencies, the team reunited and recruited Ghost Rider, Robbie Reyes, whose powers proved uniquely capable of harming Aida. As Aida and Ivanov made a final bid to incite global anti-Inhuman hysteria using the Darkhold and LMD assassins, Coulson’s team set a trap. In a climactic confrontation, Coulson (temporarily empowered as the Ghost Rider) and Robbie incinerated Aida once and for all, and the Darkhold was returned to the hellish dimension from which it came. With the Framework collapsing, Yo-Yo managed to convince Mack to return to reality, though both were emotionally devastated by the loss of Hope.

Despite defeating Aida, S.H.I.E.L.D. remained public enemy number one. Wounded but united, Coulson's team took a moment for themselves, only to be swiftly apprehended by unknown operatives wielding advanced technology that immobilized them. As they await an uncertain fate, the future of S.H.I.E.L.D. and its beleaguered heroes hangs in the balance, with Fitz still struggling with guilt, Coulson harboring a secret deal with the Spirit of Vengeance, and their names further tarnished before the world.
