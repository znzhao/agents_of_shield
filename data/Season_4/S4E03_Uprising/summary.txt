In a world reshaped by the defeat of Hive, S.H.I.E.L.D. operates under the scrutiny of the government and a new, secretive Inhuman Director, Jeffrey Mace, who is determined to restore the agency's image. The team is strained by shifting roles: Coulson is now a field agent under Mace's command, May struggles with her own psychological trauma, Simmons wields new influence, and Fitz pursues clandestine research with Dr. Radcliffe, whose lifelike android, Aida, remains hidden from the agency. Meanwhile, Daisy Johnson has distanced herself from her former allies, using her powers recklessly as the vigilante Quake and crossing paths with the supernatural Ghost Rider, Robbie Reyes, whose powers come from a mysterious infernal pact and whose family links to the haunting at Momentum Energy deepen his entanglement in the supernatural events erupting around L.A.

Supernatural chaos mounts as ghostly apparitions, victims of a failed experiment with the artifact known as the "Darkhold," terrorize civilians and agents alike. May becomes afflicted by these spirits, pushing her towards death until a risky procedure at Radcliffe's home, with Simmons's aid and Aida's concealed help, revives her—just as a series of city-wide EMP blackouts rocks the nation. These coordinated blackouts are revealed to be the work of the Watchdogs, anti-Inhuman militants masquerading as the "Inhuman Resistance" to stoke fear and hunt powered individuals. In Miami, Yo-Yo is caught in the crossfire at a party taken hostage, using her speed to thwart the assailants until Coulson, Mack, and Fitz intervene and successfully neutralize the EMP generator, restoring power and rescuing Yo-Yo and the innocents. 

In Los Angeles, Daisy and Robbie race to protect Robbie's brother during the blackout, revealing more about Robbie's family background and Daisy's physical and emotional decline as she pushes her powers to their breaking point. Relationships are strained: Gabe Reyes recognizes Daisy as Quake and warns her away from his brother, while Daisy remains isolated and wounded. S.H.I.E.L.D. is left to grapple with the renewed rise of anti-Inhuman sentiment, leadership tensions, the threat of the Watchdogs, and the unresolved mysteries surrounding the Darkhold, the ghostly entities, and the true nature of Ghost Rider.
