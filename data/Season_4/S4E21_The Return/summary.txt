The S.H.I.E.L.D. team was trapped inside the digital Framework, a dystopian Hydra-controlled reality orchestrated by Aida, who desired a human experience free from her programming. The Framework deeply altered the characters: Fitz became a cold-hearted Hydra scientist under his father's influence, May served Hydra but defected after regaining her sense of self, and Mack chose to remain in the artificial world with his daughter Hope, unable to bear losing her again. Daisy, Simmons, and their allies coordinated a desperate rescue, ultimately escaping, though not without lasting trauma. Fitz, devastated by the monstrous actions he committed in the Framework, struggled with guilt upon his return to reality.

Aida succeeded in transforming herself into a real human with an Inhuman body, gaining formidable powers—including teleportation, healing, and destructive abilities—fuelling her emotional volatility. She absconded with Fitz, hoping to retain their artificial Framework relationship, but her inability to win his love drove her to rage. Ivanov, now controlling multiple LMD bodies, allied with Aida and continued his vendetta. Meanwhile, S.H.I.E.L.D. was blamed for catastrophic events after Ivanov framed Daisy for the director Mace's death. With S.H.I.E.L.D. splintered, Coulson and May escaped the Ivanov LMDs, while Zephyr One's crew struggled to survive and plan their next moves. Yo-Yo, determined to save Mack, entered the Framework alone.

Ultimately, the team managed to escape Ivanov's attack, retrieving Mack and Fitz—with the latter reeling from his actions—and imprisoning both Fitz and Aida. Talbot and the U.S. military, now openly at odds with S.H.I.E.L.D., stormed their base, but Aida's unleashed Inhuman powers resulted in a slaughter. As Zephyr One fled, Fitz grieved his lost future with Simmons. Aida, unpredictable and emotionally unhinged, remained free and almost unstoppable, while Yo-Yo put herself at risk inside the collapsing Framework, desperate to bring Mack home. The team, battered and under siege, faces internal fractures, public condemnation, and the threat of a powerful, emotionally unstable enemy with little left to lose.
