S.H.I.E.L.D. continues to face threats in a changed world following the defeat of Hive. The agency has become a government-sanctioned entity under a mysterious new director—an enhanced Inhuman with superhuman strength who is determined to restore S.H.I.E.L.D.’s reputation and funding. Coulson, demoted to field agent, and May struggle with the agency's changes and new rules, while Simmons gains influence as an advisor to the director. Fitz secretly assists Dr. Radcliffe in developing Aida, a lifelike, sentient android built for agent protection. Meanwhile, Daisy, wracked with guilt over her actions under Hive's control and Lincoln’s sacrifice, has left the team to act as the vigilante "Quake." Her crusade to punish weapons traffickers in Los Angeles brings her into conflict with the supernatural entity Ghost Rider, embodied in Robbie Reyes—a young man who claims he sold his soul for his demonic powers.

Supernatural threats escalate as ghostly apparitions linked to a mysterious box begin haunting civilians and S.H.I.E.L.D. agents alike, inducing violent paranoia and hallucinations. Fitz, Simmons, and Mack investigate the box’s origins, tracing it to an abandoned energy lab in Pasadena. These specters, former employees like Lucy Bauer, were apparently victims of an experiment gone awry involving an artifact called the "Darkhold." Daisy and the Ghost Rider become entangled as they both search for answers, neither fully trusting the other. The chaos intensifies as Robbie and Daisy pursue the ghosts to the lab, where one of the entities attempts to trigger a catastrophic meltdown, forcing unlikely teamwork between Daisy, Fitz, Mack, and Ghost Rider to avert disaster. Daisy’s continued isolation and reckless power use take a toll on her health, while May, infected by the ghostly presence, descends into violent psychosis until the director subdues her with his superhuman strength, revealing his Inhuman nature to his closest agents.

Unresolved mysteries abound—S.H.I.E.L.D.'s new leadership and methods, the true origins and goals of the ghostly antagonists, the secrets of the "Darkhold," and the nature of Ghost Rider’s pact and powers. The team is fractured, old friendships strained, and the supernatural is on the rise, leaving S.H.I.E.L.D. scrambling to hold the line between the known and unknown.
