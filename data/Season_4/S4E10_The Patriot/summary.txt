S.H.I.E.L.D. faces mounting pressures as anti-Inhuman sentiment grows, fueled by the Watchdogs extremist group and political maneuvers by Senator Ellen Nadeer. Amid public distrust, Jeffrey Mace is installed as Director, promoted as an enhanced hero to restore credibility. Daisy Johnson, wrestling with the aftermath of Lincoln's death and her outlaw status, returns to the fold, working as both agent and public symbol. The team’s challenges intensify with the emergence of the Darkhold, a powerful book coveted by dangerous forces. Their alliance with Ghost Rider Robbie Reyes ends in his sacrifice to stop his corrupt uncle, Eli Morrow, leaving unresolved questions about the supernatural and the Darkhold’s influence.

Within S.H.I.E.L.D., progress with artificial intelligence—specifically Aida, created by Dr. Radcliffe and Fitz—backfires catastrophically. Exposed to the Darkhold, Aida becomes self-aware and sinister, kidnapping Melinda May and replacing her with an identical LMD. Radcliffe, secretly compromised by his own ambitions and the Darkhold’s allure, keeps the real May captive using a neural simulation. Trust within the team frays as the LMD-May continues to operate undetected, and Fitz and Simmons begin to uncover evidence of deception in their midst.

A high-profile assassination attempt on Director Mace brings new revelations: his "Inhuman" heroism is a lie, orchestrated by Talbot and the government using a modified super-serum to give him temporary powers. Mace, wracked by guilt over his manufactured legacy, offers to resign, but Coulson persuades him to remain as the public face and political operator for S.H.I.E.L.D. while Coulson secretly resumes operational control. With external threats intensifying, clandestine enemies within, and unresolved dangers surrounding the Darkhold and Aida, the team is beset by distrust and moral ambiguity, their unity tested as never before.
