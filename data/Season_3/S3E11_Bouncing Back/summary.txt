The world is reeling from both public fear of Inhumans and the fallout from S.H.I.E.L.D.'s clandestine war with Hydra. Phil Coulson leads his team through this volatile landscape after Hydra’s plot to resurrect an ancient Inhuman entity—a monster known as 'Hive'—comes to a head. Coulson killed Grant Ward on the alien world Maveth to prevent Hive's return, but the ancient being instead returned to Earth in Ward's body, now allying with Gideon Malick and a depleted Hydra. The fate of the world hangs in the balance as S.H.I.E.L.D. races to contain the Inhuman outbreak, manage trauma within its own ranks, and fight an enemy that may be far more formidable than anything they've faced.

Significant relationship shifts and emotional recoveries are underway. May learns her ex-husband Andrew is the Inhuman killer Lash. Simmons, freed from months on an alien planet, struggles to process her ordeal and her relationship with Fitz, who risked everything to save her. The death of Rosalind Price—Coulson’s ally and romantic interest—further hardens Coulson, as he is forced into uneasy cooperation with the US government. With the president's blessing, Coulson is positioned to covertly lead Inhuman containment efforts through the public ATCU front, while remaining in the shadows.

Meanwhile, S.H.I.E.L.D. expands its outreach, encountering and recruiting Inhumans like Joey and now Elena "Yo-Yo" Rodriguez, a Colombian woman with super speed who snaps back to her starting point. The team struggles with how to protect these new beings, fight growing anti-Inhuman sentiment, and stop both Lash and Hydra from exploiting their kind. A dire flash-forward hints at catastrophe ahead: a S.H.I.E.L.D. agent's shuttle explodes in space, foreshadowing a grim fate for at least one member. The true threat of Hive remains unknown, but its sinister resurrection has only just begun to manifest through Ward's decaying corpse.
