After the catastrophic fall of Hydra and S.H.I.E.L.D.’s public unraveling, Phil Coulson leads a reconstituted team beset by new threats and global uncertainty. The accidental release of Terrigen has triggered transformations around the world, producing a growing population of unstable Inhumans. S.H.I.E.L.D. now works covertly to locate, secure, and support these individuals before hostile forces intervene—specifically, the government’s Advanced Threat Containment Unit (ATCU), led by the determined Rosalind Price, which suspects S.H.I.E.L.D. of killing Inhumans rather than helping them. Daisy Johnson (formerly Skye), now embracing her identity and powers as an Inhuman, endeavors to unite both groups and form an autonomous team. Meanwhile, Leo Fitz grows desperate in the wake of Jemma Simmons’ mysterious disappearance into the alien Monolith, chasing leads that point to other worlds.

Pursuing the hope that Simmons survived, Fitz discovers that the Monolith is a portal to a distant planet—a conclusion drawn from impossibly ancient sand retrieved during a risky experiment. The team enlists the expertise of Professor Randolph, an Asgardian, following clues that lead them to an English castle containing ancient knowledge and technology tied to the Monolith. With Daisy’s powers helping to reproduce the conditions needed, Fitz bravely travels through the portal and miraculously rescues Simmons from the hostile alien world, reuniting her with the team. Concurrently, Lance Hunter sets out to pursue vengeance against Grant Ward, the Hydra rogue still plotting in the shadows, and recruits a reluctant but ultimately willing Agent May to join him. May, coping with trauma and time spent supporting her father, steps back into action, reinvigorated by word of Simmons’ rescue and her own sense of unfinished business. As the team celebrates small victories, unresolved threats linger: more Inhumans manifest uncontrollably, the true intentions of the ATCU remain murky, and the monstrous serial killer stalking Inhumans is still at large. The shadow of Hydra, though diminished, continues to loom, promising further danger for S.H.I.E.L.D. and its fractured allies.
