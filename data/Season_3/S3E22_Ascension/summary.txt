The threat of Hive has escalated S.H.I.E.L.D.'s war for survival. Once a formidable asset, Daisy fell under Hive's sway, instigating betrayal and heartbreak within the team before Lash's intervention freed her at great cost. Hive, exploiting Dr. Radcliffe's research, turned S.H.I.E.L.D. agents into primitive Inhumans, transforming their home into a battleground. Tensions with government oversight, particularly under the Sokovia Accords, forced S.H.I.E.L.D. into a precarious position even as they fought to stop Hive's apocalyptic agenda. Daisy's liberation left her racked with guilt and seeking redemption, while Fitz and Simmons' relationship deepened, Mack became a source of emotional support, and Elena (Yo-Yo) proved her loyalty to the cause.

Hive seized a missile silo and attempted global release of a pathogen to forcibly evolve humanity, but the team foiled the launch and captured both Hive and Radcliffe. Hive's followers enacted a backup plan, unleashing primitives inside S.H.I.E.L.D.'s base and forcing the fractured team to regroup and improvise. As Hive escaped to the Zephyr with his warhead, the agents split up—May and Fitz stowing away, Simmons blinding the primitives by raising the temperature, and Daisy grappling with a vision of sacrificial fate. Coulson's ruse distracted Hive while the team coordinated to move the warhead; when Daisy tried to sacrifice herself, Lincoln, gravely wounded, intervened. He piloted the quinjet—with the warhead and Hive—into space, sacrificing himself to destroy Hive and the threat for good. The explosion left Daisy devastated and the team mourning Lincoln's heroic final act, while Hive's menace at last came to an end. Though the immediate crisis has passed, the wounds to S.H.I.E.L.D.—and Daisy's own soul—remain unresolved.
