In the wake of S.H.I.E.L.D.’s public exposure and the fracturing of Hydra, the world is thrown into chaos by the outbreak of Terrigen Mist, awakening new Inhumans everywhere. Coulson leads the secretive rebirth of S.H.I.E.L.D., walking a tense line between protection and preemption as he forges an uneasy alliance with the government-run ATCU, led by Rosalind Price. While Daisy Johnson emerges as a fierce advocate for Inhumans, internal and external trust issues abound, with Mack and others questioning whether S.H.I.E.L.D. can keep its identity and purpose clear amid mounting paranoia about who is truly in control.

The threat of Lash—the mysterious killer of Inhumans—is revealed to be Dr. Andrew Garner, Agent May's ex-husband, whose accidental Terrigenesis pushes May into a heart-wrenching confrontation. With Andrew contained, S.H.I.E.L.D. seeks answers to deeper mysteries: Simmons’ harrowing return from a distant planet, her unresolved feelings for Fitz, and the discovery that Hydra’s ambitions stretch back millennia, tied to an ancient Inhuman exiled by the Kree. As Fitz and Simmons work to unlock the secrets of the portal, their bond is tested, culminating in a raw, emotional exchange and a long-awaited kiss.

Behind the scenes, Grant Ward violently carves his own path through the remnants of Hydra, sparring with the calculating Gideon Malick. S.H.I.E.L.D. uncovers that the ATCU, supposedly researching a cure for Inhumans, is actually working with Hydra to create more of them—led by Malick, who orchestrates Hydra’s true goal: bringing back the ancient, all-powerful Inhuman once banished to another world. Malick aligns himself with Ward, revealing that Hydra’s ultimate purpose has always been to serve this entity and that the NASA program which trapped Simmons was in service of this plan. As the pieces fall into place, S.H.I.E.L.D. realizes the scale of Hydra’s ancient conspiracy, while trust, revelations, and high-stakes infiltration converge to set the stage for a battle over the fate of both Inhumans and humanity itself.
