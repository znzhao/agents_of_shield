The struggle between S.H.I.E.L.D. and the ancient Inhuman Hive escalates as Hive, possessing Grant Ward’s body, continues his quest to create an Inhuman army and enact his vision of a new world order. Using his unique power to enslave Inhumans, Hive turned Daisy Johnson against her friends, devastating the trust within the team and allowing him to further his experiments to forcibly turn humans into Inhumans. Despite the efforts of Fitz, Simmons, and Lincoln to develop a cure against Hive’s influence, their attempts have failed, and Hive has become all but invulnerable, manipulating Dr. Radcliffe and harvesting Kree DNA from Daisy to improve his gruesome transformation experiments.

With the Sokovia Accords now enforced, S.H.I.E.L.D. faces governmental scrutiny as General Talbot arrives to document all Enhanced agents. Coulson manages Talbot’s oversight while simultaneously fighting Hive’s rapidly growing threat. A critical turning point comes when Daisy, still under Hive’s sway, helps who she believes is Lincoln escape S.H.I.E.L.D. and reach Hive’s town, only for it to be revealed that she was tricked—Lincoln was never on the plane; instead, Lash, the Inhuman killer, is sent in his place. Lash succeeds where others failed, destroying Hive’s parasites within Daisy and freeing her from his control, but he is killed by James before escaping with Daisy. Now Daisy, guilt-ridden but liberated from Hive, returns to S.H.I.E.L.D., and Hive is left with his monstrous new creations and an undeterred commitment to his apocalyptic plan.

Meanwhile, personal relationships deepen amidst the chaos: FitzSimmons finally confess their love, Elena (Yo-Yo) grows closer to the team, and the fracturing effect of Hive’s manipulations lingers. Despite hard-won victories, major mysteries remain: what Hive’s true endgame entails, whether S.H.I.E.L.D. can develop an antitoxin, and if the reunited team can stop Hive’s plan before the world is overrun by Inhumans. The pressure mounts as the fate of the agency and humanity hangs in the balance.
