S.H.I.E.L.D. has been locked in a desperate struggle against Hydra and the ancient Inhuman Hive, who possesses the body of Grant Ward. Hive’s ability to infect and control other Inhumans has created a crisis of trust—especially after Daisy Johnson, under his sway, betrayed the team, murdered Gideon Malick, and escaped with valuable resources, devastating the base with her quake powers. The Secret Warriors have been fractured by suspicion, and Lincoln’s determination to save Daisy is checked by Coulson’s restrictive measures due to the risk of Hive’s influence spreading.

As the team reels from Daisy’s defection, they refocus on finding a way to break Hive’s hold. Fitz and Simmons, driven by hope and personal connection, track down Dr. Holden Radcliffe in Romania, hoping his expertise with invasive species and biology can produce a cure. Their mission is fraught with danger as Daisy and Hive are also recruiting and converting other Inhumans—Alisha and James (now Hellfire), using Terrigenesis and Hive’s parasite to build their own army. Daisy remains fiercely loyal to Hive, threatening her former teammates and warning Fitz about a vision foretelling the death of a S.H.I.E.L.D. agent.

Coulson and May narrowly escape a trap set by Hive’s forces and regroup as S.H.I.E.L.D., with help from Talbot’s military ops, begins to dismantle Hydra’s infrastructure—final acts spurred by Malick’s information. Meanwhile, FitzSimmons’ partnership grows deeper following their harrowing encounter with Hive’s forces, culminating in newfound intimacy. Hive now possesses the full Kree artifact, believed capable of destroying him, and his plans for an Inhuman "utopia" under his control take shape. S.H.I.E.L.D. remains fragmented and vulnerable, racing against time to save Daisy and the world from Hive’s escalating threat.
