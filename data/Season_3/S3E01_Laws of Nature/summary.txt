Following Hydra's exposure and the internal collapse of S.H.I.E.L.D., Phil Coulson and his team have rebuilt the organization from the shadows, navigating new threats and fraying alliances. The reveal of the Inhumans—a secret race with superhuman abilities awakened by Terrigen—is a seismic event, drawing both interest and fear from world powers. After Jiaying's attempt to unleash Terrigen on humanity and the tragic fallout that ended in her death, Skye—now going by Daisy Johnson—positions herself at the forefront of a task force proactively seeking, rescuing, and guiding new Inhumans. Coulson, permanently altered after losing his hand, leads a battered but resilient S.H.I.E.L.D., while remnants of HYDRA stir under Grant Ward. Bobbi Morse has recovered physically but is still sidelined from fieldwork following her injuries, while Leo Fitz is consumed by the loss of Jemma Simmons, who vanished into a mysterious artifact known as the Monolith.

With Terrigen spreading steadily across the globe, incidents of unprepared Inhumans emerging and losing control have become more frequent. S.H.I.E.L.D. races to contain and support these individuals before government or other hostile forces can intervene. Their latest rescue is Joey Gutierrez, a disoriented new Inhuman with the power to liquefy metal, whom Daisy, Mack, and Hunter save from a deadly government black ops team led by the enigmatic Rosalind Price. Price's unit, working under a new government directive known as the Advanced Threat Containment Unit, believes S.H.I.E.L.D. is killing Inhumans outright—a misunderstanding worsened by evidence of Inhumans being found dead, killed by a mysterious third party using energy blasts.

Amid this turmoil, Lincoln Campbell refuses to rejoin S.H.I.E.L.D., feeling betrayed, while Daisy struggles to bridge the gap between her Inhuman family and her S.H.I.E.L.D. loyalty. Fitz, obsessed with rescuing Simmons, goes to dangerous lengths, even obtaining a cryptic scroll that offers no answers—only the word "death." The team is split between new threats, personal losses, and mounting mistrust from the government. As President Ellis makes a public call for a new approach to superhuman threats, S.H.I.E.L.D. faces fresh scrutiny and the emergence of a monstrous, unidentified Inhuman killer. With alliances fragile and mysteries deepening—especially Simmons' fate inside the Monolith—the team braces for unpredictable dangers ahead.
