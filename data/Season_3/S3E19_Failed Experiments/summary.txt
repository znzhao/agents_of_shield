Throughout the escalating conflict between S.H.I.E.L.D. and the ancient Inhuman Hive (inhabiting Grant Ward’s body), the team has suffered both physical and emotional losses. Hive’s unique ability to infect and mentally enslave other Inhumans led to the betrayal of Daisy Johnson, who devastated the base, murdered Gideon Malick, and severely fractured S.H.I.E.L.D.'s trust. While Coulson and May dismantled the remaining Hydra infrastructure, Fitz and Simmons tracked down Dr. Holden Radcliffe, hoping his knowledge could help develop a cure for Hive’s parasitic control, but also discovered that Hive’s plans involve converting vast numbers of humans into Inhumans. Daisy and other infected Inhumans, such as Alisha and James (Hellfire), joined Hive, growing his power and ambitions for an Inhuman “utopia.” The FitzSimmons partnership deepened amidst the chaos, culminating in newfound intimacy despite the looming catastrophe.

Hive revealed his tragic origin as the first Inhuman, created by Kree experimentation, and set about re-creating this process with Radcliffe’s unwilling assistance. Attempts to transform Hydra’s loyalists failed fatally, forcing Hive to summon actual Kree Reapers to harvest the necessary DNA. S.H.I.E.L.D. attempted a risky assault on Hive’s base in Wyoming to rescue Daisy and stop Hive’s experiment, only to face Kree invaders and be forced into a retreat after suffering casualties and learning of Hive’s apparent invulnerability. Mack’s desperate attempt to reach Daisy resulted in a painful confrontation, proving that Hive’s grip on her is absolute. Meanwhile, despite developing a potential antitoxin, Fitz, Simmons, and Lincoln’s hopes were dashed when the cure failed its first test, leaving S.H.I.E.L.D. with no clear strategy to rescue Daisy or defeat Hive, who now seems all but unstoppable.
