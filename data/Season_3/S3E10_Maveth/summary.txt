The world has been thrust into chaos following the exposure of S.H.I.E.L.D. and Hydra and a sudden surge of Inhuman awakenings triggered by Terrigen Mist. As Coulson leads S.H.I.E.L.D. through uneasy alliances with government forces led by Rosalind Price, the team faces not only external threats but also internal turmoil: May discovers her ex-husband Andrew is the murderous Inhuman Lash; Simmons is rescued after months on a mysterious, deadly alien world but struggles with trauma and unresolved feelings, especially as Fitz’s devotion to her deepens. Meanwhile, Hydra—under the direction of Gideon Malick and Grant Ward—pursues its ancient goal: returning a legendary, monstrous Inhuman from the world Simmons escaped.

After Ward murders Rosalind, Coulson is overcome by grief and vengeance. Hydra succeeds in capturing Fitz and Simmons, forcing Fitz to help open a portal to the alien planet, Maveth. S.H.I.E.L.D. launches a daring assault on Hydra’s stronghold, but Simmons is recaptured and only escapes with Lash’s help—who, in turn, slaughters the captive Inhumans. On Maveth, Fitz and Ward ally uneasily to find the returning portal, and discover "Will"—but Fitz soon realizes "Will" is dead, his body possessed by the ancient entity Hydra worships. As the creature attempts to return to Earth, Coulson intervenes: he kills Ward in cold blood, and with Fitz’s help, thwarts the entity’s plan by destroying its host body. The team barely escapes the destruction of the Hydra castle, regrouping on Zephyr One, traumatized but united by their near loss and shocking discoveries. The true nature of Hydra’s ancient agenda and the threat of the entity persist, as the team mourns friends lost and fatefully changed relationships.
