Hydra, revitalized under the control of an ancient Inhuman entity now possessing Grant Ward's body—known as "Hive"—has orchestrated a series of moves to assert global influence, leveraging Inhuman abilities and deep political infiltration. Gideon Malick, Hydra’s principal power broker, is revealed to carry a legacy of both religious zealotry and personal betrayal. Through flashbacks, it's shown that as a boy, Malick manipulated the sacred lottery during Hydra's human sacrifice ceremony, leading to the death of his own brother Nathaniel, a secret he has kept hidden for decades. This betrayal comes back to haunt Malick when Hive, having inherited Nathaniel's memories, psychologically torments him and ultimately murders Malick's daughter Stephanie to force Gideon to understand the meaning of true sacrifice.

Within S.H.I.E.L.D., trust is fraying amid internal and external threats. Bobbi Morse and Lance Hunter were exiled after a mission compromise, leaving the core team reeling and short-handed. The public, whipped into panic by attacks from the anti-Inhuman extremist Watchdogs, demands crackdowns on powered individuals. May mourns the permanent transformation of her ex-husband Andrew Garner into Lash, while Daisy Johnson emerges as a leader and champion for Inhumans, recruiting allies like Yo-Yo Rodriguez and Lincoln Campbell, and resisting attempts to use a newly developed Inhuman "cure." Daisy and Lincoln’s mission to consult with James, a volatile Inhuman exile with ancient Kree knowledge, uncovers lore about Hive—real name "Alveus"—and the Kree’s original purposes for creating Inhumans. Lincoln’s confession of his troubled past deepens his bond with Daisy, even as they recognize the escalating threat Hive poses.

Meanwhile, S.H.I.E.L.D.'s pursuit of Hydra’s technological advances and exploitation of Inhuman powers nearly proves disastrous. During a confrontation at a Transia Corporation facility, Giyera, a Hydra agent with telekinetic abilities, infiltrates S.H.I.E.L.D.’s ranks and seizes the team’s airborne base, setting up their capture. Simmons discovers that Hive’s monstrous abilities are rooted in parasitic organisms—he is not just a host, but a colony, resurrecting Ward’s body as a vessel. With most of S.H.I.E.L.D. incapacitated, Daisy and Lincoln urgently prepare to assemble the Secret Warriors, signaling the need for all their powered allies in the face of Hydra’s ancient, inhumanly powerful menace. The lines between loyalty, destiny, and sacrifice—both personal and collective—have never been more sharply drawn.
