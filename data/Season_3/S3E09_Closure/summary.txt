After the public exposure of S.H.I.E.L.D. and Hydra’s fragmentation, the accidental spread of Terrigen Mist has triggered the emergence of Inhumans worldwide, casting the world into uncertainty and fear. Coulson and S.H.I.E.L.D. strive to adapt, forging a fragile partnership with the government-controlled ATCU under Rosalind Price, even as trust issues and hidden agendas simmer. The core team wrestles with their own growing fractures, highlighted by May’s agony upon discovering that Lash—the Inhuman hunting his own kind—is her ex-husband Andrew Garner. Simmons’ ordeal on a distant planet, her fraught, evolving relationship with Fitz, and the uncovering of a Kree-linked alien entity at the center of Hydra’s centuries-old purpose fuel both emotional and existential stakes for the group.

Coulson’s alliance with Rosalind deepens into romance, only for Ward—a Hydra operative now aligned with Gideon Malick—to assassinate her, devastating Coulson and reigniting his personal vendetta. Ward and Malick work to open a portal to the alien world, seeking to bring back the ancient Inhuman. Meanwhile, Fitz and Simmons are captured by Hydra in a gambit to wrest secrets of portal traversal, with Hydra using torture and manipulation. Driven by grief and fury, Coulson kidnaps Ward’s estranged brother Thomas to bait Ward and tracks Hydra’s location to England’s castle, while delegating S.H.I.E.L.D.'s command to Mack. The fate of the team, the threat of Hydra’s plan, and the possible return of a world-ending Inhuman now hang in the balance, with lives and loyalties more vulnerable than ever.
