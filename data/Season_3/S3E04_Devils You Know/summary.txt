In the wake of Hydra’s collapse and S.H.I.E.L.D.’s exposure, Phil Coulson leads his team in a dangerous new world where Inhumans are emerging due to a widespread Terrigen outbreak. S.H.I.E.L.D. works secretly to find, protect, and support Inhumans, but faces competition and mistrust from the government-run ATCU, led by Rosalind Price. Daisy Johnson strives to rally newly awakened Inhumans but faces obstacles as some resist help and others are hunted. The mysterious Inhuman killer, now revealed as "Lash," systematically murders other Inhumans, leaving S.H.I.E.L.D. scrambling for answers and struggling to keep their people safe.

Leo Fitz successfully rescues Jemma Simmons from a hostile alien planet, though she returns deeply traumatized, her experiences and the planet’s mysteries unresolved. Meanwhile, Mack and Daisy uncover Lash’s method for tracking Inhumans: a virus planted via email, leveraged by Dwight Frye, a tormented Inhuman collaborator. The subsequent joint operation with the ATCU leads to a disastrous ambush by Lash, further cementing him as a frightening, possibly ideologically-driven threat after Daisy glimpses him shifting into a human silhouette. Alisha’s attempt to recruit fellow Inhumans ends in tragedy, underscoring the constant threat they all face.

Hydra, under Grant Ward's ruthless new leadership, regroups and escalates its violence. Lance Hunter and Melinda May wage a risky undercover campaign to assassinate Ward, leading to a confrontation where Ward manipulates them—targeting May’s ex-husband, Dr. Andrew Garner, to secure his escape. A deadly stand-off leaves the fate of Dr. Garner uncertain: his life hangs in the balance after Hydra attacks him, and May is left distraught and desperate for answers. Unresolved tensions simmer between S.H.I.E.L.D. and the ATCU, the true aims of both organizations remain clouded, and the threat of uncontrolled Inhuman activity continues to grow as Lash’s campaign intensifies and Simmons struggles with the aftereffects of her ordeal.
