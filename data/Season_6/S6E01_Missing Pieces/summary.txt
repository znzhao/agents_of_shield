Following a cataclysmic series of events, S.H.I.E.L.D. barely managed to avert the Earth's destruction at the hands of the gravitonium-powered General Talbot, though this victory cost the team dearly: Fitz died heroically, Simmons was left bereft, Deke disappeared mysteriously (possibly erased from existence), and Coulson, terminally ill, spent his final days away from the team. The aftermath left Mack stepping up as the new director, while the group as a whole fractured, responding to trauma and loss in ways that have permanently altered their dynamics and relationships.

One year later, the team is split between Earth and a desperate search for Fitz in deep space. Daisy, Simmons, Piper, and Davis scour the galaxy, following clues left by the fate of Enoch and Fitz's cryo-pod after their ship was destroyed. Simmons, obsessive and driven by hope and guilt, risks the team's safety, overriding the decision to return home in favor of chasing a lead on Fitz's possible whereabouts. Meanwhile, on Earth, Mack leads a rebuilt S.H.I.E.L.D. alongside May and new allies, investigating mysterious reality distortions linked to ancient laylines. During a breach at the Museum of Natural History in Indiana, S.H.I.E.L.D. confronts a dangerous group (Jaco and his accomplices) who arrive through a dimensional portal, led by a man who is an uncanny double of the late Phil Coulson, but claims total ignorance of S.H.I.E.L.D. and executes an agent without hesitation. The nature of these new threats—including the enigmatic "Pachakutiq," the secrets behind the reality breaches, and the true identity and intentions of Coulson's doppelgänger—remain unresolved as S.H.I.E.L.D. faces a new era of uncertainty and peril.
