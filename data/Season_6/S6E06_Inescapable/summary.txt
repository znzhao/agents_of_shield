Following the battle with Graviton, S.H.I.E.L.D. has been left weakened and divided. On Earth, Mack leads the organization as it confronts a new threat: Sarge, a mysterious man identical in appearance to the late Phil Coulson, but ruthless and unfeeling. Sarge and his team wage a relentless war against the Shrike, parasitic creatures who infest and crystallize human hosts, paving the way for their world-ending master, Pachakutiq. May survives being captured by Sarge, gradually uncovering the catastrophic scope of the Shrike invasion, while the emotional toll from recent losses, particularly among Yo-Yo and Dr. Benson, puts the team under strain. Dr. Benson's investigations tie the Shrike's crystals to the shattered monoliths—objects that hint at terrifying power over life, death, and possibly reality itself.

Simultaneously, in deep space, Daisy, Simmons, and their group are seized by the Chronicoms, led by Atarah, whose home planet has been destroyed. Atarah demands the secret of time travel to save her people, taking Simmons hostage and forcing Fitz, who survived his own harrowing trials, to cooperate. Daisy, Piper, and Davis are left stranded as Simmons sacrifices her freedom. Inside a Chronicom mental prison, Fitz and Simmons are confronted with their deepest traumas and relationship scars, including the truth about Fitz's alternate self's marriage to Simmons and Coulson's death. Their psychological odyssey, facing manifestations of their guilt, fear, and villainous alter egos, ultimately leads to reconciliation and a renewed love for each other, culminating in an acceptance of their haunted pasts. With unexpected help from Enoch—their ever-loyal Chronicom ally—they finally break free, but are immediately launched deeper into a fraught and uncertain future as Simmons reveals a shocking personal secret to Fitz: he is to become a grandfather.

S.H.I.E.L.D. remains beset by external threats and internal wounds. The nature of Sarge's identity, the true danger posed by the Shrike, the Chronicoms’ pursuit of time travel, and the universe-shaking power of the monoliths all remain unresolved. The agents are scattered and fighting for survival on multiple fronts, their bonds tested by grief, love, and the weight of impending cataclysm.
