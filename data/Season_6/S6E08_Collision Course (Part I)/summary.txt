After the death of Phil Coulson, S.H.I.E.L.D. struggles for leadership and cohesion as a new, world-ending threat emerges. Mack leads the fractured organization while contending with Sarge, a mysterious figure who is Coulson’s double but with ruthless intent. Sarge and his team—Snowflake, Jaco, and Pax—hunt the Shrike, deadly parasitic creatures whose infection crystallizes victims and who herald planetary extinction for the demon known as Pachakutiq. Jaco reveals their tragic backstory, having lost his own world and family to these forces, mirroring Yo-Yo’s grief after Keller’s death. May, Yo-Yo, and Dr. Benson study the Shrike and their links to the ancient monoliths, suspecting that their destruction set these new threats in motion. S.H.I.E.L.D. is forced into a dangerous alliance with Sarge, whose abrasive methods and growing hold over some agents create deep internal divisions.

Meanwhile, Fitz and Simmons are reunited in deep space and, with Daisy and the team, become pawns in the Chronicoms’ bid to save their erased homeworld, facing mind prisons and painful reckonings until they break free. A mysterious woman, Izel, intervenes and shepherds FitzSimmons back toward Earth, revealing she seeks the three Monoliths—the "Di'Allas"—stolen from her world, devices with the power to bridge space and time. Izel's true nature emerges as she infects her crew with Shrike and manipulates FitzSimmons for her agenda, revealing that a man hunted her for her knowledge and was behind the annihilation of the Chronicoms’ planet.

On Earth, Sarge leads a convoy to intercept Izel as she approaches, planning to annihilate her, the Shrike, and a chunk of the planet itself with a bomb set at the alien tower now rising. Tensions escalate as Mack tries to maintain oversight, forcing Deke into Sarge’s crew to repair vital equipment. Relationships strain further: Daisy and May question Sarge’s commitment, Snowflake develops a fixation on Deke, and former loyalty lines blur. In the episode’s climax, Sarge betrays Snowflake, locks himself in his truck, and uses a portal device to escape to the Zephyr, leaving May and Daisy scrambling to stop a cataclysmic detonation even as the Shrike tower threatens the world. Loyalties are tested, the nature and motivations of both Sarge and Izel remain deeply mysterious, and S.H.I.E.L.D. faces its most existential threat yet—one that is both cosmic and achingly personal.
