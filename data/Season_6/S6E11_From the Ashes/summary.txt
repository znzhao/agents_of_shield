S.H.I.E.L.D. reels from the true loss of Phil Coulson, only to face a deeper mystery: the arrival of Sarge, a hardened leader with Coulson’s face and fragments of his memories, but a ruthless, alien-driven purpose. Sarge's crusade brings him and his team into opposition with Izel, a non-corporeal being who creates and commands parasitic Shrike, aiming to open a gateway for her people using the energy of the monoliths. S.H.I.E.L.D.'s struggle turns desperate as Izel reveals the ability to possess human hosts, manipulating agents to further her plan while sowing distrust within their ranks. Loyalty is tested and relationships strained: FitzSimmons navigate grief and return from forced interstellar separation, while Yo-Yo, Mack, and May all wrestle with guilt, loss, and questions of identity. May, haunted by her connection to Coulson, ultimately kills Sarge, but he survives and his true nature is uncovered—he is Pachakutiq, an entity born of the monoliths' creation energy as a mirror to Coulson, sharing his memories but not his soul.

Now, Izel forcibly extracts the location of a hidden temple from Dr. Benson by threatening to torment him with the reanimated memory of his late husband, demonstrating her mastery over the monoliths’ creation energy. Yo-Yo and Mack, taken captive on Zephyr One, engineer a ploy that allows Mack to jettison the gravitonium device and help S.H.I.E.L.D. track them. Meanwhile, Sarge’s struggle with his identity intensifies: Daisy pushes him to embrace his true self by forcing a near-death transformation, which endows him with unstable superhuman power and deepens his existential torment. Daisy, seeing the remnants of Coulson in Sarge’s plea and memories, chooses compassion over execution, bolstering Sarge’s desire for purpose. Deke realizes that Izel’s harmonic manipulation can be disrupted by the frequencies emitted from Sarge’s sword, suggesting a tangible weapon to counter her. As S.H.I.E.L.D. races to decipher Izel's endgame and avert an invasion, the fates of May, Daisy, Sarge, Mack, Elena, and FitzSimmons are intertwined by grief, hope, and the specter of creation itself, with the future of reality hanging by a thread.
