S.H.I.E.L.D., still reeling from the true and final loss of Phil Coulson, faces threats both personal and cosmic. The arrival of Sarge—a brutal, mysterious leader bearing Coulson’s face but not his morals—splinters loyalties within the team. Sarge battles the Shrike, parasitic creatures engineered by the alien Izel, who seeks the Di'Allas (Monoliths) to bridge worlds and bring disaster to Earth. As S.H.I.E.L.D. races to protect their home and the universe, relationships are strained and old wounds reopened—FitzSimmons return after being manipulated by Izel in space, while May and Yo-Yo struggle with their grief and the question of identity. Multiple sacrifices and betrayals unfold: Jaco gives his life to destroy the Shrike, Sarge murders his own crew in paranoia, and May, haunted by her history with Coulson, kills Sarge in custody.

However, May’s out-of-character actions reveal a greater horror—Izel is discovered to be able to possess bodies, using S.H.I.E.L.D. agents as pawns to further her agenda. The team is forced into distrustful lockdown, revealing secrets to prove their identities while Izel leaps from host to host, leaving death in her wake—including the murder of Davis. Sarge, meanwhile, survives May’s attack and heals rapidly, evidence that he is not human. Fitz deduces that the Monoliths’ destruction—previously believed to have unleashed a "fear dimension"—instead released the energy of creation, inadvertently spawning Sarge as a copy of Coulson, with his memories and face but a different soul. Izel and Sarge are revealed to be non-corporeal beings from another reality, Sarge truly named Pachakutiq, and their intertwined destinies threaten to unleash a catastrophic invasion. S.H.I.E.L.D. stands battered and divided, their enemies now inside the walls and the very nature of reality itself at stake.
