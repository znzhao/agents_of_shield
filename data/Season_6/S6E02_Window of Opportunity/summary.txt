After the catastrophic defeat of General Talbot, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team suffered immense losses: Fitz died heroically, leaving Simmons heartbroken and obsessively searching for a way to reunite with him, while Coulson, facing his mortality, spent his final days away from the agency before succumbing to his illness. The team has since split, with Simmons, Daisy, Piper, and Davis scouring the cosmos in pursuit of Fitz, following cryptic clues about his fate. Meanwhile on Earth, Mack leads a rebuilt S.H.I.E.L.D. alongside May and new allies, contending with mysterious reality distortions linked to dimensional breaches.

A pressing threat emerges when a group led by a man identical to Coulson, calling himself Sarge, begins carrying out high-tech heists on Earth. Sarge and his lethal crew—Jaco, Snowflake, and Pax—show no hesitation to kill and possess advanced technology unfamiliar to S.H.I.E.L.D. Their goals seem to revolve around acquiring ‘P.E.G.s,’ crystal-like power sources, and their activities point to sinister motives. S.H.I.E.L.D., reeling from the murder of Agent Fox at Sarge’s hands, is thrown into confusion by his impossible appearance. May is adamant about Coulson’s death, yet Dr. Benson’s analysis confirms that Sarge is a perfect genetic match for Coulson, deepening the enigma. As May confronts Sarge and his crew during a robbery, she is outmaneuvered and captured, and S.H.I.E.L.D. learns—through recordings from Sarge's fallen comrade—that wherever this mysterious group appears, destruction follows, hinting that Sarge and his team may be harbingers of planetary doom. Unresolved questions swirl around Sarge's origins, his connection to Coulson, and the true nature of the threat now facing Earth.
