Following the death of Phil Coulson, S.H.I.E.L.D. has been torn by leadership struggles and existential threats. Mack leads the organization against the enigmatic Sarge, a ruthless but Coulson-like figure, whose mission to stop the alien Shrike has caused uneasy alliances and fractures within S.H.I.E.L.D. The Shrike, horrific entities infecting victims and heralding planetary extinction, were engineered by the mysterious Izel, who manipulates FitzSimmons in deep space in her quest for the Monoliths—the Di'Allas, devices bridging space and time. Sarge’s team—Jaco, Pax, and Snowflake—bear scars from losing their own world to the Shrike, creating tragic parallels with the grief-stricken agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.

As Izel brings FitzSimmons back to Earth, Sarge’s plan to annihilate her and the Shrike risks global catastrophe, culminating in a desperate race to neutralize an atomic bomb hurtling into the Shrike-infested tower. Daisy averts an immediate disaster by containing the explosive with her powers, but the revelation that Izel can possess and command the Shrike renders victory direly incomplete. On the Zephyr, Sarge and his crew seize control, but shifting loyalties emerge as Jaco and Yo-Yo help S.H.I.E.L.D. turn the tables—though Sarge executes Pax out of anger and suspicion. After a daring rescue operation, Jaco sacrifices himself by detonating the bomb on Izel’s ship, destroying the Shrike and granting S.H.I.E.L.D. a hard-fought reprieve.

With FitzSimmons safely returned, the team briefly celebrates, rekindling old relationships and honoring fallen allies. Yet as Sarge is contained, May—grappling with unresolved trauma and Sarge’s uncanny resemblance to Coulson—takes matters into her own hands and shoots Sarge in his cell. Despite the apparent defeat of the Shrike and Izel’s ship, her whereabouts and the deeper nature of Sarge and Izel remain unresolved, leaving S.H.I.E.L.D. facing lingering mysteries and threats that are both intimate and cosmic.
