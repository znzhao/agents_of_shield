S.H.I.E.L.D. lies fractured across space and Earth in the wake of the battle with Graviton. On Earth, Mack leads a battered organization coping with the enigmatic threat of Sarge—a man with the identical face of the late Phil Coulson, but none of his compassion. Sarge leads a ruthless team hunting hosts of parasitic creatures known as Shrike, which infect and crystallize victims from the inside out. Sarge claims a century of experience and reveals his mission: to exterminate the Shrike before their master, Pachakutiq—"the death of everything"—destroys another world. May, captured and tested by Sarge, learns firsthand of the Shrike's horrifying effects and barely escapes, seizing control of Sarge's truck after a brutal struggle. Within S.H.I.E.L.D., the deaths and emotional fallout—exemplified by Yo-Yo’s grief for Agent Keller—strain already fragile bonds, while Dr. Benson's study of Shrike-related crystals reveals connections to the destroyed monoliths, hinting at powers over life and death.

Meanwhile, in space, Daisy, Simmons, Piper, and Davis are captured by a Chronicom fleet led by Atarah, who reveals that the Chronicom homeworld was destroyed. The Chronicoms seek to master time travel to avert their extinction, coercing Simmons into captivity and forcing Fitz, still alive but in enemy hands, to work on their behalf. Daisy's attempt to rescue everyone leads to a standoff, and Simmons sacrifices her freedom for her team’s safety. The fate of the agents remains uncertain—Daisy, Piper, and Davis released but separated from Simmons, while Fitz and Enoch’s survival hinges on the Chronicoms’ desperate gambit. S.H.I.E.L.D. remains divided, haunted by the mysteries of Sarge’s true identity, the nature of the Shrike threat, and whether Earth is merely the next victim in a cosmic cycle of destruction.
