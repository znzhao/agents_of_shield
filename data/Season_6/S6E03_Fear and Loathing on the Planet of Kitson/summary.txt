After the devastating loss of Fitz and the impending death of Coulson, the S.H.I.E.L.D. team fractured into two fronts: one group led by Daisy, Simmons, Piper, and Davis has traveled deep into space in a desperate and obsessive search for Fitz, whose apparent death left Simmons emotionally driven and willing to risk everything; the other, grounded on Earth and led by Mack and May, fights to maintain order in the face of bizarre dimensional phenomena and a new, existential threat. The Earth team is rocked when a man identical to Phil Coulson, calling himself Sarge, emerges as the ruthless leader of a crew perpetrating violent, mysterious robberies with advanced tech and seemingly apocalyptic aims, deepening the mystery of his impossible existence.

Meanwhile, Simmons’s single-minded focus strains her relationships, particularly with Daisy and the rest of the space team, as they bounce from tip to tip across the galaxy. Their journey leads them to the lawless planet Kitson, following clues that Fitz (who is alive after all, journeying with Enoch the Chronicom) has been diverted there. On Kitson, Fitz and Enoch fall into peril when Enoch's cover is blown and both are jailed; Fitz only narrowly escapes thanks to Enoch's ingenuity. Daisy, Simmons, and Piper, accidentally intoxicated by alien hallucinogens, attempt a chaotic rescue, only for Malachi, an advanced "hunter" seeking both Fitz and Enoch, to snatch Fitz away at the last instant. This leaves Simmons distraught, her team in disarray, and Fitz's fate once again uncertain. The new threats surrounding S.H.I.E.L.D., from the enigma of Sarge on Earth to Malachi's relentless pursuit across the stars, signal mounting dangers with reality-spanning consequences, as old bonds are tested and hope grows ever more tenuous.
