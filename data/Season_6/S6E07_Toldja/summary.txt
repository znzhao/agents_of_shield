S.H.I.E.L.D. has been left fractured by loss and uncertainty after the defeat of Graviton and the death of Director Phil Coulson. On Earth, Mack leads the organization as it faces a deadly new menace: Sarge, a mysterious stranger who is Coulson's physical double but possesses a ruthless drive. Sarge and his team—Snowflake, Jaco, and Pax—are hunting the Shrike, parasitic aliens whose infection crystallizes human hosts and heralds planetary destruction by the world-ending Pachakutiq. Sarge's methods are violent and enigmatic, making uneasy allies of S.H.I.E.L.D. as May, Yo-Yo, and Benson investigate the Shrike and their link to the shattered monoliths—ancient artifacts with ties to time, space, and reality-warping power. Jaco, taken captive, reveals that he—a former baker—lost his planet and family to the Shrike, further deepening the emotional toll among the agents, especially Yo-Yo, who is still grieving Keller.

Meanwhile, Daisy, Simmons, and their team ventured into deep space in search of Fitz, only to be captured by the Chronicoms led by Atarah. Desperate to restore their destroyed home planet, the Chronicoms demand the secret of time travel, forcing Fitz and Simmons into a mental prison where they confront painful truths about their past, Coulson's loss, and their relationship. With Enoch's assistance, FitzSimmons escape, only to be transported back to the casino planet Kitson by a malfunctioning Chronicom disc. There, they become fugitives again and are forced into a deadly gambling gauntlet by Mr. Kitson until an enigmatic woman, Izel, intervenes, seeking their help in retrieving something stolen from her—drawn to their expertise with Earth.

Back on Earth, Sarge and his team are imprisoned at the Lighthouse, but when the Shrike infection in S.H.I.E.L.D. custody becomes critical, Sarge uses his knowledge to secure leverage and semi-control, helping avoid a catastrophe by revealing that the Shrike can be neutralized by high altitude and cold. Mack, desperate and outmaneuvered, acquiesces to Sarge's demands, as S.H.I.E.L.D.'s personnel start to look to Sarge for leadership. The agents remain scattered and on edge: bonds are fraying, loyalties tested, and mysteries—Sarge’s true identity, the monoliths, the nature of Pachakutiq, and the Chronicoms’ quest—remain unresolved as new and unexpected threats emerge.
